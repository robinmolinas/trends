## Overview

GLP-1 receptor agonists (glucagon-like peptide-1 inhibitors) are a class of drugs originally developed for type 2 diabetes that have become transformative weight-loss and metabolic health tools. Key brands: Ozempic and Wegovy (Novo Nordisk). As of 2025–2026, their implications extend well beyond obesity treatment into a broader longevity and healthspan story.

## Role in Trends

- [[wiki/concepts/coming-of-age]] — potentially the single most consequential consumer health technology in the Coming of Age trend; enabling active aging by addressing obesity as the root cause of many age-related conditions

## Key Facts

- GLP-1 drugs can halve the risk of heart attack
- Linked to reduced risk of 42 conditions: dementia, addiction, certain cancers, heart disease, and more
- US obesity rates may have peaked in 2020 and are now declining — potentially reversing a decades-long trend — attributed to these drugs
- 14% of people say they would "definitely" use a GLP-1 inhibitor if easily available at affordable price; 28% say they "might" — a very large addressable market
- Novo Nordisk (maker of Ozempic) is working on broader accessibility and affordability
- Democratization lag remains: affluent, urban, insured populations benefit first

## Tensions

- Access and affordability are the primary constraints on societal impact. If these drugs remain expensive, they become a longevity privilege for the wealthy.
- Long-term effects of widespread GLP-1 use on population health, food systems, and consumer markets (notably food and beverage) are not yet fully understood.
