## Summary
McKinsey CPG Practice 8-page article (January 2025) on gen AI in the beauty industry. The core argument: gen AI could add $9–10B to the global economy through its beauty industry impact alone, but most players are testing, not scaling. Four use cases to prioritize, plus organizational and governance imperatives for scaling AI from experiment to competitive advantage.

## Key Claims
- Gen AI could add **$9–10B to the global economy** through beauty industry impact alone
- The gap between laggard and leader beauty companies will grow significantly once leaders deploy gen AI at scale — "the fast become faster"
- **Four priority use cases**: (1) Speed to market for product development and content, (2) Consumer experience personalization, (3) Supply chain and demand forecasting, (4) Trend identification and competitive intelligence
- AI enables rapid content creation at scale while remaining responsive to fast-changing consumer tastes (Mondelēz cited as cross-category example using gen AI for personalized content)
- Organizations must customize gen AI to their specific beauty data — generic tools are insufficient for category-specific competitive advantage
- Long-term imperatives: governance structures, IP protection, consumer trust standards, regulatory watch (particularly for skincare health claims)

## Data Points
