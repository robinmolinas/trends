## Summary

Dentsu's 16th annual Media Trends report (45 pages) argues that in a landscape of guaranteed uncertainty, brands should anchor strategy in three invariable human truths: we are simple until we are complex; we are social animals; we don't read advertising. Each truth maps to a cluster of 2026 media trends.

Part 1 (We Are Simple Until We Are Complex): Search experience optimization (GEO/SEO convergence), digital delegation (AI agents taking over consumer tasks), and the friction paradox (zero friction is not always optimal — the right amount of friction fosters community and trust). Dentsu explicitly identifies the Labubu frenzy as proof that "the thrill of the chase and the fear of missing out are powerful drivers."

Part 2 (We Are Social Animals): Communities shape narrative (niche interest communities outperform broadcast), shared memories hit different (live events, shared cultural moments amplify brand resonance), business messaging takes off (WhatsApp/iMessage as commerce channel).

Part 3 (We Don't Read Advertising): AI-generated audience insight can unlock deeper consumer understanding, but human oversight is the entire point — AI without governance produces generic, stereotyped outputs at scale. Attention economics are evolving beyond click metrics.

## Key Claims

- Three enduring human truths: we seek simplicity; we are social animals; we don't read advertising
- GEO (generative engine optimization) is replacing SEO as primary discovery mechanism
- AI agents will handle increasing amounts of consumer decision-making — "digital delegation"
- The friction paradox: convenience is valuable but zero-friction erodes community, choice, and trust
- Niche communities of interest outperform broadcast media for brand affinity
- Live shared experiences create disproportionate brand resonance
- AI-generated insights are only as good as the underlying data — most AI tools draw on old, biased, unsegmented public web data
- 84% of US advertisers and marketers are using AI professionally (GWI survey)
- Social media is far from dead — it remains the dominant media in daily lives

## Data Points

- 84% of US advertisers/marketers using AI professionally (GWI survey)
- 1 in 2 business decisions still made without consumer insights input despite known value
- AI tools now rank #3 most used input for business decision-making (leapfrogging whitepapers, social listening)
