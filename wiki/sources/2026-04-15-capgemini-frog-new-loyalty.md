## Summary
Published by frog (part of Capgemini Invent), this 18-page report synthesizes three structural failures in personalization and introduces "New Loyalty" as the framework for rebuilding consumer trust. The core finding: 2024 was the worst customer experience in a decade for US consumers (Forrester CX Index lowest since 2016), driven by pandemic-era cost-cutting that reduced personalization to conversion optimization at the expense of brand loyalty.

## Key Claims
- **2024 was the worst US customer experience in a decade**: Forrester's 2024 Customer Experience Index dropped to its lowest level since 2016. Cross-industry CX score experienced consistent decline since pandemic.
- **The experience gap is structural**: Brands shifted to cost reduction and conversion optimization during lean times, undermining the "effectiveness-ease-emotion" triad that drives loyalty.
- **Old loyalty programs are placeholder personalization**: Loyalty programs became default stand-ins for genuine personalization — points mechanics without relationship depth.
- **New Loyalty requires "known and understood"**: Consumers want brands to know them holistically and act proactively, not just recognize purchase history.
- **True personalization = intersecting data + emotional relevance**: New Loyalty requires consolidated zero-party, first-party, and contextual data (location, time, device, weather, prior interactions) into intelligent consumer profiles.
- **Physical + digital integration**: Genuine personalization requires supply chain alignment — AI recommendations must connect to real-time availability, not just algorithmic preference.

## Data Points
- Forrester 2024 CX Index: lowest US consumer experience score since 2016
- Report establishes that CX decline correlates with conversion-optimization priority over loyalty investment
- Three CX dimensions measured: Effectiveness (meets needs?), Ease (easy to work with?), Emotion (how it made you feel?)
- Published by frog/Capgemini Invent, citing Forrester data
