## Summary

Moore's M.Cast Trends Report applies a "futurist's lens" to business and culture, targeting decision-makers navigating uncertainty. The lead trend is "Invisible AI" — AI becoming so seamlessly integrated into operations that it essentially disappears, mirroring how the web became invisible infrastructure in the mid-2000s. The parallel is precise: businesses once had "web departments" and "internet strategies"; AI is following the identical trajectory from experimental project to embedded business intelligence.

A second core thread is the human response: as AI becomes transparent infrastructure, the remaining differentiators are distinctly human — strategic communication, creative thinking, and authentic brand storytelling that embraces imperfection as proof of humanity. The organizations that win are those forging genuine community connections and cutting through information overload with intentional communication.

## Key Claims

- AI is following the internet's trajectory: from "Now on the Web!" novelty → invisible infrastructure
- 34% of organizations now use embedded AI as their primary deployment method vs only 19% favoring standalone apps (McKinsey State of AI)
- 78% of organizations use AI in at least one business function, but only 1% describe rollouts as "mature"
- The invisible AI trend signals AI's evolution from disruptive technology to essential business capability
- Human differentiators post-AI: strategic communication, authentic storytelling, genuine community connection
- "Imperfection as proof of humanity" is becoming a brand strategy — embrace human flaws as signal of authenticity
