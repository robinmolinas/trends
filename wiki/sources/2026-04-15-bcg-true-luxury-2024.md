## Summary
BCG's 10th edition of the True-Luxury Global Consumer Insights study (12,000 respondents, quantitative + 1,000 deep-dive interviews with ultra-high spenders averaging €350K/year). This year's edition focuses on "Beyond Money" customers — those spending >€50K/year personally on luxury goods and experiences. The study finds that Beyond Money consumers (< 1% of clients, but 21% of luxury spend) have increasingly distinctive motivations requiring completely different brand strategies than the broader aspirational market.

## Key Claims
- **Luxury pyramid stratification intensifying**: Beyond Money (<1% of clients) = 21% of 2023 luxury spend (up from 12% in 2013)
- **Beyond Money profile**: Average spend ~€350K/year; each is worth ~230× one aspirational consumer
- **Luxury market structure 2023**: Beyond Money (0.6M people, €213B); Top Absolute (1.7M, €44B); Entry Absolute (16.4M, €109B)
- **Beyond Money motivations are post-materialist**: Status, product acquisition, and badge signaling matter less. Craft mastery, cultural access, unique experiences, and co-creation with brands dominate
- **Client Advisors as strategic assets**: For Beyond Money clients, the human advisor relationship is irreplaceable — AI and digitization are supplements, not substitutes
- **Three new thresholds introduced**: Beyond Money (>€50K), Uber Money (€300K-1M), and Ultra Money (>€1M) — each requiring distinct relationship models
- **Geographies shift**: China remains key but individual brand growth harder; Middle East, India, and other emerging markets growing in share

## Data Points
- Study: 12,000 quantitative respondents across 12 luxury markets; 1,000 deep-dive Beyond Money (<€50K avg/year); 20 VIC 1:1 interviews; 15 VIC focus groups in 3 regions; 20 CEO/executive interviews
- Beyond Money = 0.6M people globally (2023), generating €213B luxury spend
- Top Money = 1.7M people, €44B spend
- Entry Absolute = 16.4M people, €109B spend
- Beyond Money share of luxury spend: 12% (2013) → 21% (2023)
- Threshold: €39K average spend per year for True Luxury consumers

- The 10th edition's focus on Beyond Money may over-represent the ultra-wealthy at the expense of the much larger aspirational market that determines brand health for most luxury companies
- "Client Advisors as strategic assets" conflicts with luxury brands' ongoing digitization and cost-reduction initiatives
