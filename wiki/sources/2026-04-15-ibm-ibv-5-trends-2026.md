## Summary
IBM Institute for Business Value's 2026 trends report (surveying 1,000+ C-suite executives via Phronesis Partners and 8,500 global consumers/employees via Suzy) argues that organizations must develop an "appetite for ambiguity" — seeking out cracks in existing markets rather than seeking stability. Five trends define the 2026 operating environment: uncertainty as competitive asset, employees wanting more AI, customers holding AI accountable, global AI resilience requiring local safety nets, and quantum advantage demanding coalition-building.

This is a distinct IBM report from the previously ingested IBM IBV AI & enterprise trends report, covering broader strategic trends including quantum computing timelines and geopolitical AI fragmentation.

## Key Claims
- **Trend 1 — Uncertainty as asset**: 74% of executives say economic/geopolitical volatility will create new business opportunities in 2026; 96% say highest-stakes decisions made in 2025 turned out to be right; 95% say they must increasingly make fast decisions
- **Trend 2 — Employees want more AI**: At least 2× as many workers across age groups say they would embrace — rather than resist — greater AI use by employers in 2026; 81% of employees are confident they can keep up with future workplace AI advances
- **Trend 3 — Customers hold AI accountable**: Easy-to-understand explanations of how AI uses consumer data is the top comfort driver; trust and transparency as product differentiators
- **Trend 4 — Global AI resilience requires local safety nets**: AI infrastructure fragmentation by geography and geopolitics; organizations need regional redundancy in AI systems separate from global dependencies
- **Trend 5 — Quantum advantage demands strength in numbers**: Quantum computing advantage will arrive through multi-party ecosystems and consortia rather than individual breakthroughs
- Only 34% of executives are optimistic about the global economy (but 85% are optimistic about their own organization's performance)
- 61% of executives optimistic about their regional economy; 59% optimistic about their own industry

## Data Points
- Survey: 1,000+ C-suite executives (Phronesis Partners) + 8,500 global consumers/employees (Suzy), 2025
- 84% positive outlook on their organization's future performance despite macro uncertainty
- 74% see economic/geopolitical volatility as creating new opportunities in 2026
