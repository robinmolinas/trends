## Summary
Commissioned by Omio (global travel search/booking platform, 80,000+ tickets daily, 430+ staff), The Future Laboratory's 33-page report maps five future travel scenarios for 2035. Four drivers shape the future of journeys: World Openness, Trust in Transparency, Accelerating Technologies, and Infrastructural Impacts. The report's frame is that travel will shift from physical point-to-point logistics to identity-expressive, AI-curated, multimodal experiences.

## Key Claims
- **Five Future Journey Types by 2035:**
  1. **Travel-tainment**: Journeys as immersive entertainment experiences — the trip is the destination
  2. **Identity Itineraries**: Travel as self-expression and identity construction — journeys curated to express who you are
  3. **Intuitive Agents**: AI agents autonomously planning, booking, and managing travel on behalf of consumers
  4. **Easy Escapes**: Frictionless, low-effort micro-travel and spontaneous getaways enabled by AI and transport infrastructure
  5. **Intermodal Advantage**: Seamless multi-modal journeys (train → ferry → autonomous vehicle) as a competitive differentiator

- **World Openness**: Geopolitical barriers vs. consumer desire for global mobility — a growing tension
- **Trust in Transparency**: Travelers demand real-time environmental impact data, honest operator communication, visible supply chains
- **Accelerating Technologies**: Autonomous vehicles, AI agents, biometric seamlessness, and AR overlays transforming the journey experience
- **Infrastructural Impacts**: Investment gaps and grid constraints limiting physical transport expansion even as digital capabilities race ahead

## Data Points
- Omio sells 80,000+ tickets daily; operates across Europe, US, Canada, Southeast Asia
- 430+ staff from 50+ countries; offices in Berlin, Prague, Melbourne, London, Bangalore
- Research methodology: Future Laboratory proprietary Foresight Research System (FRS) + expert interviews
- Experts: Dr Dimitrios Tsivrikos (UCL consumer psychology), Rohit Talwar (futurist), Roya Zeitoune (YouTube EMEA culture/trends)
