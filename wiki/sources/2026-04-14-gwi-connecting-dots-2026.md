## Summary

GWI's annual non-obvious trends report draws on 2M+ annual surveys (GWI Core, USA, Kids, Zeitgeist panels) to surface what mainstream marketing plans are likely missing. The five trends: AI making insights generic and stereotypical; the intent-behavior gap (consumers contradict themselves); social media is dominant (not dying); the 2026 World Cup is serious but fans aren't who you think; Gen Alpha is turning sixteen and must be taken seriously.

The AI-and-insights finding is the most actionable: 84% of US advertisers use AI professionally, and AI tools have become the third most-used input for decision-making. But AI tools draw on public web data that is old, inconsistent, biased, and unsegmented. The result: AI-generated marketing insights are producing stereotyped, generic conclusions at scale. The real insight requires human judgment applied to high-quality, current, proprietary data — not just AI applied to public noise.

On Gen Alpha: 50%+ of respondents' children began their digital journey by age 5; 77% by age 7. They are being raised in a fundamentally different information environment than any prior generation. The 2026 World Cup (US/Canada/Mexico) will attract a new audience profile — younger, more global, less traditional football fan demographic.

## Key Claims

- 84% of US advertisers/marketers use AI professionally; AI is now #3 most used input for decisions
- AI tools draw on biased, old, unsegmented public web data — producing generic, stereotyped insights at scale
- 1 in 2 business decisions still made without consumer insight despite known value — friction, not skepticism
- Social media is not dying — it is the dominant media channel in daily life
- World Cup 2026 (US/Canada/Mexico) will be massive and fan profile will surprise brands who rely on historical soccer demographics
- Gen Alpha turning 16: 50%+ began digital journey by age 5; 77% by age 7 — first generation to be truly native to algorithmically mediated reality
- Consumers are more contradictory than ever: stated intent and actual behavior increasingly diverge

## Data Points

- 84% US advertisers/marketers using AI professionally (bespoke GWI survey, 550 professionals, Aug–Sep 2025)
- GWI Core: 1M+ annual surveys across 54 markets
- World Cup 2026 data: GWI Zeitgeist survey, September 2025, 11 markets
