## Summary

WTW's annual Global Medical Trends survey (based on responses from insurers across all major global markets) projects a sustained and worsening medical cost inflation environment for 2026. Global gross medical trend is forecast at 10.3% in 2026 (up from 10.0% in 2025 and 9.5% in 2024). Fifty-six percent of insurers expect further cost increases, and 55% expect elevated costs to persist for more than three years.

The structural drivers are consistent across regions: new medical technologies (cited by 74% of insurers globally as the primary driver), decline of public health systems (52%), and pharmaceutical advancements (49%). Cancer is now the fastest-growing and most expensive condition driving claims globally, with 75% of insurers observing increased cancer incidence among individuals under 40. GLP-1 coverage is concentrated in the Americas (55% have it in their plans), with 68% of global insurers expecting GLP-1 use to increase — and with it, associated costs.

Spotlight topics include behavioral health, fertility/family planning, cancer (particularly early-onset), GLP-1 integration into insurance plans, and AI in healthcare.

## Key Claims

- Global gross medical trend: 9.5% (2024) → 10.0% (2025) → 10.3% (2026)
- Asia Pacific highest: 14.0% projected in 2026 (up from 13.2% in 2025)
- Latin America sharpest acceleration: 10.5% (2025) → 11.9% (2026)
- 56% of insurers anticipate further increases in global medical cost trends
- 55% expect elevated cost levels to persist for more than three years
- New medical technologies cited by 74% of insurers globally as #1 cost driver
- Decline of public health systems cited by 52%; pharmaceutical advancements by 49%
- Cancer is the fastest-growing condition driving claims in the Americas (69%), Asia Pacific (58%), and Europe (54%)
- 75% of insurers observe increased cancer incidence among individuals under 40
- Early-onset cancer incidence rose 79.1% globally between 1990–2019 (BMJ Oncology)
- GLP-1 coverage: 55% of Americas insurers have it; only 2 in 5 globally
- 68% of insurers globally expect GLP-1 use to increase; 73% in Americas; 77% in Europe
- GLP-1 costs for obesity expected to increase over the next three years
- Fraud, waste, and abuse is the #1 cost driver in Middle East and Africa (cited by 79% of insurers)
- Behavioral health ranks 3rd globally among conditions driving medical claims costs

## Data Points

- Survey base: insurer respondents across all major global markets (WTW proprietary)
- US medical trend: 9.6% gross in 2026 (net of inflation: 7.2%)
- India: 12.9% gross medical trend projected for 2026
- Indonesia: 15.1% gross; China: 11.1% gross for 2026
- Early-onset cancer deaths increased 27.7% between 1990–2019 (BMJ Oncology, 2023)

## Contradictions / Tensions

- GLP-1 coverage is highly uneven globally — US/Americas far ahead of other regions. Insurers globally expect use to grow, but coverage infrastructure is not in place in most markets — widening the health equity gap.
- Sustained medical inflation above GDP growth in most regions is structurally unsustainable. 55% of insurers expect 3+ years of elevated costs, which implies either benefit cuts or premium increases that will further stress household finances.
