## Summary

Adobe's annual Creative Trends 2026 report (developed through research with internal Adobe data, external data, and feedback from Adobe Creative Cloud communities) identifies four visual styles and aesthetic trends shaping the creative landscape for 2026. The overarching frame: while 2025 was marked by excitement around AI image generation, 2026 represents a "thoughtful blend of innovation and intention." Creative content needs are growing 5x–20x, driven by AI tools that enable faster production — but the risk is losing the human meaning that makes creative work resonate.

The four trends: (1) All the Feels — multisensory visuals that engage more than sight (texture, taste, sound); (2) Connectioneering — creative work that builds human connection and community; (3) Surreal Silliness — humor, absurdity, and joy as brand strategy; (4) Local Flavor — hyperlocal authenticity and cultural specificity as a counterweight to globalized sameness. Together they reflect a creative landscape responding to AI commoditization by doubling down on human emotional resonance.

## Key Claims

- 95.2% of content leaders consider visual content critical to business communications (Adobe research)
- Content needs are growing 5x–20x — driven by AI tools enabling faster production at scale
- 82% of global consumers expect as many senses as possible to be engaged when experiencing something new (The Drum)
- 65% of customers want brands to "wow them" with out-of-the-box campaigns
- 49% are more likely to buy from brands that make them feel joy (WGSN)
- Four visual trend themes: All the Feels, Connectioneering, Surreal Silliness, Local Flavor
- AI enables faster creative production but risks eroding the "heart of what makes work meaningful"
- Most forward-thinking teams are building systems that integrate people, ideas, and workflows — not just adding AI tools
- Human-made creative work is resurging as a premium signal in the face of AI commoditization

## Data Points

- Visual content importance: 95.2% of content leaders call it critical (Adobe proprietary)
- Content demand growth: 5x–20x due to AI production enablement
- Sensory engagement: 82% of consumers want multi-sense engagement (The Drum)
- Joy-driven purchases: 49% more likely to buy from joy-invoking brands (WGSN)

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Adobe's business model depends on creative professionals using their AI tools (Firefly), yet the report implicitly warns against over-reliance on AI at the expense of human meaning. This is a careful balancing act — promote the tools while reassuring creatives about relevance.
- Local Flavor (cultural specificity) is in tension with AI-generated content, which tends toward global averages and trained biases. The trend toward hyperlocal authenticity is a reaction to AI's homogenization tendency.
