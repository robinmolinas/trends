## Summary

BCD Meetings & Events' annual "What's Trending" report for 2026 provides a global MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, Events) industry outlook. The framing: "the true value of meetings and events lies in human connection." The report covers MICE trends by region (NORAM, UK, EMEA, APAC, LATAM) plus thematic sections on: destinations, meetings management, collective approaches, venue search evolution, and AI/tech adoption.

Three headline trends for MICE 2026: (1) AI-driven personalization transforming attendee engagement; (2) predictive analytics enabling smarter, more effective event formats; (3) sustainability becoming foundational with tools to measure and offset impact. BCD is explicit that meetings are increasingly treated as "strategic assets" with spending aligned more closely with business objectives — moving the MICE category from expense to investment.

The BCD/Minor Hotels partnership section reveals MICE's growing strategic importance: over a quarter of Minor Hotels' global revenue comes from MICE business, described as offering "rare stability, predictability and long-term value" compared to volatile B2C demand. This positions corporate events as a resilience strategy for hospitality operators.

## Key Claims

- Meetings increasingly treated as "strategic assets" aligned with business objectives, not discretionary events
- Three MICE trends for 2026: AI personalization, predictive analytics, sustainability measurement
- MICE represents >25% of Minor Hotels' global revenue — a strategic stability driver vs. B2C volatility
- AI-powered booking tools and streamlined processes are being deployed across the MICE stack
- Immersive experiences and thoughtful design are setting new quality standards
- Economic pressure, sustainability requirements, and geopolitical complexity are all influencing event decisions
- BCD's "Thinking Forward" three-year plan (launched 2024) is in its second year of execution
- Technology adoption in MICE is the "burning issue" flagged across all regional markets

## Data Points
