## Summary

J.P. Morgan Asset Management's 2026 Year-Ahead Investment Outlook ("AI Lift and Economic Drift") frames 2026 as a year of resilience beneath the surface contradictions. The headline: U.S. economic growth will show a "cold, hot, cold again" pattern driven by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) stimulus creating a first-half surge, followed by deceleration as tariff impacts pass through consumer prices and immigration constraints suppress labor supply.

The K-shaped economy is the defining macro frame: upper-income households are benefiting from equity wealth effects and AI-linked spending; lower- and middle-income households remain under pressure from housing costs and inflation. This divergence shapes investment strategy — structural themes (AI infrastructure, deregulation, international earnings convergence) outperform cyclical ones.

AI investment is a critical growth driver: data center capex is running at 1.2–1.3% of U.S. GDP; 44% of U.S. firms now pay for some AI model or platform; 9% report AI integration in production. But the report is clear that AI valuations need earnings delivery — "bubbly but underpinned by solid fundamentals." The key risk: if AI capex boom loses momentum, it could trigger a recession given how much wealth effect and business investment is AI-driven.

## Key Claims

- U.S. real GDP growth: ~1% Q4 2025, surging to above 3% in H1 2026, then declining to 1–2% by late 2026
- CPI inflation peaks below 4% in June 2026, then falls to 2% by year-end
- Fed expected to cut rates 2–3 times through 2026 (shallow easing path)
- K-shaped economy: upper-income households benefiting from equity gains; lower/middle income squeezed
- AI capex now 1.2–1.3% of U.S. GDP — historically significant scale
- 44% of U.S. firms paying for an AI model or platform; 9% report AI in production
- OBBBA fiscal stimulus: full expensing of equipment/R&D plus household tax refunds effective early 2026
- Job growth constrained to ~50,000/month by immigration restrictions limiting labor supply
- Tariff risk: U.S. effective tariff rate at highest since 1934 — inflation pass-through expected in H1 2026
- International equities (especially Asian emerging markets, Europe value) have further upside
- U.S. tech valuations described as "bubbly but underpinned by solid fundamentals"
- Biggest risk: AI boom "losing luster" — earnings miss or power/materials supply crunch could trigger recession
- Data center capex: $449B in projected run rate (as of reporting period)

## Data Points

- Data center capex: 1.2–1.3% of U.S. GDP; compound growth rate visible in $80B → $106B → $141B → $449B trajectory
- U.S. firms using AI: 44% pay for AI models; 9% report AI in production
- Tariff revenue: averaging >$29B/month between June–October (substantial)
- OBBBA: first-ever full expensing of equipment + R&D investment

## Contradictions / Tensions

- J.P. Morgan calls AI valuations "bubbly but fundamentally sound" — a tension with Goldman Sachs' similar framing. Both are defending AI while acknowledging bubble risk. This consensus may itself be a contrarian signal.
- The K-shaped economy is expected to persist: investment strategy explicitly bifurcates between structural (quality, AI, international) and cyclical (avoid). Middle-market companies face structural headwinds.
