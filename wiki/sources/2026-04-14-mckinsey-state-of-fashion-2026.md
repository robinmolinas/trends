## Summary

The BoF-McKinsey State of Fashion 2026 report (10th annual edition) is framed by a single phrase: "When the Rules Change." Fashion executives are contending with a fundamentally new reality — U.S. tariffs have redrawn trade maps; consumers are redirecting spending toward health and wellbeing; and AI has moved from competitive edge to business necessity. The annual BoF-McKinsey Executive Survey finds that 46% of executives expect industry conditions to worsen in 2026 (up from 39% in last year's survey), with "challenging" replacing "uncertainty" as the most-used word to describe the year.

Ten themes structure the report: (1) Tariff Turbulence — forced supply chain redesign; (2) Workforce Rewired — AI reshaping jobs toward higher-value creative and analytical work; (3) The AI Shopper — LLMs as search/recommendation engines; (4) Jewellery Sparkles — strong volume demand through 2028; (5) Smart Frames — smart eyewear as fastest-growing accessory category; (6) The Wellbeing Era — consumers prioritizing health budgets over fashion; (7) Efficiency Unlocked; (8) Resale Sprint; (9) The Elevation Game; (10) Luxury Recalibrated — luxury houses in reinvention mode after price-hiking without quality improvement.

The mid-market is the fastest-growing segment, having succeeded in elevating products and store experiences while luxury overshot on price. Agentic commerce is the next frontier: fashion brands' presence in AI chatbot responses is "the new SEO."

## Key Claims

- 46% of fashion executives expect industry conditions to worsen in 2026 (up from 39% in 2025)
- 36% view North America as unpromising or very unpromising — double last year's share
- China sentiment improving: 28% view it as unpromising in 2026 (down from 41% in 2025)
- AI has shifted from "competitive edge" to "business necessity" for fashion companies
- Agentic AI is reshaping how people work and collaborate across fashion organizations
- Customers are using LLMs to search for products, compare offerings, and receive tailored style recommendations — making AI chatbot presence the new SEO
- Mid-market is the fastest-growing fashion segment — succeeded by elevating products while luxury overshot on price
- Luxury now in "reinvention" mode — multiple major luxury houses hired new creative directors in 2025
- Jewellery: strong volume demand expected through 2028 (driven by self-gifting and self-expression)
- Smart eyewear: fastest-growing accessory category — further launches expected in 2026
- Customer loyalty/retention cited as key theme by >50% of fashion executives for 2026
- Agentic commerce expected to accelerate in the second half of the decade

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Luxury overshot on pricing without quality improvements — now in reinvention. The mid-market benefited directly. This is a structural pendulum that will likely swing back when luxury successfully reinvents, creating a boom/bust cycle for mid-market players.
- AI is described as both "business necessity" and "threat to creativity." McKinsey's frame says AI handles analytics and routing while creativity is protected, but the job displacement signal (Workforce Rewired theme) suggests the protection of creativity is not guaranteed.
