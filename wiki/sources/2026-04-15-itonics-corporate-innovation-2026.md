## Summary
ITONICS (innovation management platform, 500+ clients) distills 1,000+ cross-industry leadership conversations from 2025 into six defining shifts in corporate innovation. The core argument: after years of "proving creativity," innovation functions must now prove measurable commercial value. Economic turbulence exposed AI pilots as decoupled from business strategy — most large organizations run 20–40 AI experiments without a clear picture of combined strategic impact.

## Key Claims
- **Trend 1 — AI coordination as strategic asset**: Most organizations run 20–40 AI experiments but cannot articulate combined strategic impact or prevent duplication across business units. Governance and coordination replace scattered experimentation.
- **Trend 2 — Return on Augmented Innovation (ROAI)**: AI augments innovation team capacity; success measured by speed, evidence, and business impact rather than raw idea count.
- **Trend 3 — Unified Innovation PMOs**: Visibility is centralized (governance), while execution remains distributed in business units. Dedicated innovation functions being absorbed into R&D, IT, and strategy teams.
- **Trend 4 — Modular partnership model**: Flexible, portfolio-based partnerships replacing rigid long-term alliances. Partners selected for specific innovation sprints rather than locked relationships.
- **Trend 5 — Portfolio decisions reconnect with market signals**: Innovation portfolios being recalibrated against real customer and market feedback, not internal roadmaps.
- **Trend 6 — Innovation as competitive differentiator in tough conditions**: Only when innovation is embedded in business decisions (not positioned alongside them) does it create value; fewer than 30% of organizations successfully translate innovation into core business or scalable revenue streams.

## Data Points
- Source: ITONICS, based on 1,000+ conversations across R&D, strategy, and innovation leaders in 2025
- Fewer than 30% of organizations successfully translate innovation into core business or scalable revenue streams
- Most large organizations run 20–40 AI experiments simultaneously
- Innovation PMOs emerging as backbone of governance and visibility
- CFOs tightening portfolio discipline: innovation budgets face unprecedented scrutiny
