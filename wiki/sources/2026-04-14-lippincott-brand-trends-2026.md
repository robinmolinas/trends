## Summary

Lippincott's (brand strategy and design consultancy) annual brand/marketing trends report for 2026 is organized around 12 themes spanning brand strategy, customer experience, design, and marketing. The report is anchored in the observation that 2025 was a year of "seismic change" — AI rewrote the marketing playbook; trust emerged as the new currency in brand growth; and entry-level roles took a structural hit. Rather than celebrating AI's upside, Lippincott takes a more balanced view: AI backlash is building, "human-made" is resurging as a premium signal, and brand strategy must navigate a portfolio of AI-enabled and human-crafted outputs.

Key themes: (1) AI — The New Scarlet Letters (AI backlash accelerating; "human-made" as premium badge); (2) Play as Community Builder — physical collectibles (Labubu, Starbucks "bearista") driving community and brand affinity; (3) Self-Aware Segment — consumers who know they're being tracked and are gaming algorithms intentionally; (4) and 9 additional themes in brand, design and experience. The report uses specific brand case studies throughout: Cracker Barrel logo reversal, Gap Gen Z success, OpenAI's first brand push, the Coldplay kiss cam incident as cultural signal.

## Key Claims

- AI moved beyond buzzwords in 2025 to "rewrite the marketing playbook" — transforming operations, content, supply chains, and sales
- Entry-level roles took a structural hit in 2025 — AI displacement is not theoretical, it is happening
- Trust emerged as the new currency in brand growth in 2025
- AI backlash is building: consumers are vandalizing "dystopian AI ads" and celebrating the ability to toggle off AI content on Pinterest
- "Human-made" is resurging as a badge of honor and a driver of price premiums for knowledge workers and craftspeople
- Collectible physical goods (Labubu, Starbucks bearista) are fusing play, design, and community — brand hype objects with secondary-market value
- Consumers know when they're being tracked; self-aware consumers are gaming algorithms to get the offers they want
- Brands must build "a balanced portfolio of products, content and experiences — whether by man or machine, the key is to create with taste, conviction and intention"
- Labubu and Lulu (Pop Mart collectibles) cited as evidence that physical goods can build community and brand affinity at scale

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Lippincott warns that "AI backlash will be swift and fierce" yet also frames the future as building a balanced AI/human portfolio. These are not contradictory, but the report underestimates how hard it is to maintain creative integrity under cost and speed pressure — most brands will lean AI regardless.
- The collectibles/play trend (Labubu, bearistas) is positioned as organic community-building, but these are manufactured scarcity plays by brands. The community is real; the "authenticity" is constructed.
