## Summary

A calendar-oriented piece from Ecomondo and Renewable Matter identifying 10 sustainability trends and key events for 2026. Less analytical than ERM or IMD, but useful as an events register and policy timeline. The overarching framing: a growing gap between economic fundamentals and climate policies; governments shifting priorities toward national security and technological leadership, while markets reward transition solutions that already work and remain cautious toward immature technologies.

## Key Claims

- 2026 is the International Year of Rangelands and Pastoralists (UN declaration), focused on sustainable land management and pastoral ecosystem restoration
- The High Seas Treaty enters into force January 17, 2026, following ratification in September 2024 — extending marine protected areas beyond national jurisdictions
- Colombia hosts the first international conference on Just Transition Away from Fossil Fuels (April 28-29, 2026) with 80+ supporting states
- COP17 on Biodiversity held October 19-30, 2026 in Yerevan, Armenia — assessing progress on the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework
- COP31 (UN Climate Change Conference) held November 9-20, 2026 in Antalya, Turkey — expected to be a "reset" after COP30's disorganized proceedings in Brazil
- CBAM fully enters into force January 1, 2026 — importers must buy certificates for embedded carbon in steel, aluminium, cement, fertilisers, hydrogen, electricity
- EU RESourceEU Action Plan: €3B to reduce dependence on Chinese critical raw materials; European Critical Raw Materials Centre to be established in early 2026
- Physical climate risk assets exposed to >20% losses could increase fivefold by 2050 (MSCI); insurance premiums against physical risks rising up to 50% by 2030
- Product-as-a-Service (PaaS) rapidly gaining ground as a circular economy strategic lever

## Data Points

- CBAM enforcement date: January 1, 2026 (CBAM sectors: steel, aluminium, cement, fertilisers, hydrogen, electricity)
- €3B: EU RESourceEU Action Plan for critical minerals
- 5x: Projected increase in assets exposed to >20% climate losses by 2050 (MSCI)
- 50%: Projected rise in physical risk insurance premiums by 2030
- Coffee prices: +40% in 2024 due to climate; cocoa prices +400% in recent years as West Africa heatwaves become 10x more likely
