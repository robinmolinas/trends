## Summary
M.Cast (Moore's professional services network) applies a futurist lens to identify trends shaping business and culture for decision-makers. The 2026 report is built in collaboration with Moore partners and thought leaders. Its central thesis: as AI becomes "invisible infrastructure" embedded in everyday operations, human competitive advantages will center on strategic communication, creative thinking, authentic brand storytelling, and community connection. Imperfection becomes a signal of humanity — and a brand asset.

## Key Claims
- **The Invisible AI Revolution**: AI in 2026 is not about flashier models or more powerful capabilities — it's about AI becoming so seamlessly integrated into business operations that it disappears. "Invisible AI" represents the maturation from experimental technology to embedded infrastructure.
- **Human skills as the new moat**: Organizations' edge lies in capabilities AI cannot replicate — strategic communication, creative thinking, authentic storytelling that embraces imperfection as proof of humanity.
- **Community as the distribution channel**: Success belongs to those who forge genuine community connections, cut through information overload with intentional communication, and deliver precisely personalized experiences.
- **Imperfection as brand asset**: In a world of AI-generated content, human error, roughness, and idiosyncrasy become authenticity signals — premium markers in an era of synthetic polish.

## Data Points

- Designed for C-suite decision-makers navigating uncertainty and complexity (no specific sample size disclosed)
