## Summary
Accenture's Point of View (12 pages) argues the beauty industry is undergoing its largest structural transformation in 20–30 years, driven by consumers shifting from aesthetics to science-based, health-led, life-centric products. Traditional beauty (cosmetics, aesthetics) is losing category leadership to health and wellness convergence — the total global health and wellness market will nearly double to $3.1T by 2030, with beauty accounting for approximately one-third (~$0.9T of the $3.1T).

## Key Claims
- Consumers are driving rapid transformation: people focused on products enhancing health and wellness "from the inside out" — portfolio must center on science, not just aesthetics
- Traditional beauty will no longer lead the category — portfolio leadership passes to science-based and health-integrated products
- H&W market breakdown by 2030 ($3.1T total): Nutrition $0.90T; Active Lifestyle $0.81T; Beauty $0.83T; Mental Wellness $0.02T; Relaxation $0.13T; Treatments $0.37T (vs. 2022: $1.7T total)
- Beauty in H&W: $0.50T in 2022 → $0.83T by 2030 (66% growth)
- Opportunities require fundamental portfolio change as the "traditional beauty" definition expands to absorb devices and non-invasive aesthetic treatments

## Data Points
- Global H&W market: $1.7T (2022) → $3.1T (2030), nearly doubling
- Beauty's share of H&W: ~$0.50T (2022) → ~$0.83T (2030)
- Beauty sector = ~1/3 of total H&W market by 2030
- Accenture positions this as the "largest change in past 20–30 years" in beauty
- Source: 12-page Accenture Point of View document
