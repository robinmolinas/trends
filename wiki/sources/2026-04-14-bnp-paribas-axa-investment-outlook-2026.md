## Summary

BNP Paribas Asset Management and AXA Investment Managers' combined Investment Outlook 2026 ("The Shifting Investment Landscape") marks the first joint publication since the merger of the two firms — creating one of Europe's largest asset managers with €1.6 trillion AUM. The report identifies four themes as having potential to shape the decade ahead: geopolitics, innovation (AI), demographics, and environment.

The macro thesis: global economy enters 2026 with "surprising resilience" — IMF revised global growth projections upward to 3.2% for 2025. Europe is regaining momentum, supported by Germany's fiscal reset and reduced policy uncertainty. The U.S. faces "policy crosscurrents" — tariff-driven inflation and loose fiscal stance (OBBBA) creating pressures the Fed may not fully counter, given its labor market focus. China is expected to moderate further, relying on targeted stimulus and advanced manufacturing investment.

A strong sustainability thread runs through the report: BNP Paribas/AXA IM is committed to ESG-integrated investment, framing climate change, biodiversity loss, and rising inequality as long-term capital risk factors, not optional add-ons. European strategic autonomy (defense, digital infrastructure, industrial policy) is identified as a long-term investable theme.

## Key Claims

- Global economy enters 2026 with surprising resilience; IMF projects 3.2% global growth in 2025
- Four decade-shaping themes: geopolitics, AI/innovation, demographics, environment
- Europe regaining momentum via Germany's fiscal reset and reduced policy uncertainty
- US faces inflation pressure from tariffs and OBBBA; Fed focused on labor market risk, not inflation
- China moderating — targeted stimulus + advanced manufacturing, not consumer-led growth
- Declining birthrates in developed countries and China creating retirement system funding gaps
- AI transforming productivity, investment, and energy use — central innovation theme
- European strategic autonomy (defense + digital) as long-term investment opportunity
- Sustainability risks (climate, biodiversity, inequality) will "redefine risk in capital markets"
- Fixed income to benefit from continued central bank easing; lower rates anticipated in US and Europe
- US technology company earnings continue to drive global equity markets
- Combined AUM of BNP Paribas AM + AXA IM: €1.6 trillion as of September 2025

## Data Points

- BNP Paribas AM + AXA IM combined AUM: €1.6 trillion (as of September 30, 2025)
- Long-term savings AUM: €850 billion; Alternatives AUM: €300 billion
- IMF global growth forecast 2025: 3.2% (upward revision)

## Contradictions / Tensions

- BNP Paribas/AXA IM maintains strong ESG commitment while operating in a market environment where ESG skepticism has grown — particularly in the U.S. market. The report frames sustainability as financial risk management, which may be more defensible than values-based framing.
- The merger itself (combining BNP Paribas AM + AXA IM) creates a European asset management champion — a consolidation move that signals European financial firms are competing with scale, not just returns.
