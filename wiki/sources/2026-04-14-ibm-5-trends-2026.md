## Summary

IBM IBV's annual C-suite forecast (1,000+ executives + 8,500 consumers/employees, conducted with Phronesis and Suzy) identifies five trends shaped by the intersection of economic uncertainty, AI, and quantum computing. The report's meta-thesis: uncertainty itself is the strategic asset. Rather than seeking stability, forward-thinking leaders are finding "fissures" — cracks in existing markets where new openings emerge. The organizations best positioned for 2026 are those that have built appetite for ambiguity rather than waiting for clarity.

84% of executives have a positive outlook on their own organization's 2026 performance even though only 34% are optimistic about the global economy — a revealing split between micro-confidence and macro-uncertainty. Employees mirror this: 81% are confident they'll keep pace with future technology, but nearly half are concerned their role will be obsolete by 2030.

The most surprising finding: employees want *more* AI, not less. Across all age groups, at least 2x more workers say they would embrace greater AI use by their employer than would resist it. The narrative of AI-resistant workforces is being challenged by IBM's data.

## Key Claims

- 74% of executives say economic/geopolitical volatility will create new business opportunities in 2026
- 84% of executives have positive outlook on their own organization's performance (only 34% optimistic on global economy)
- At least 2x more workers *embrace* greater AI use than *resist* it — across all age groups
- 81% of employees confident they can keep up with future technology advances
- 61% of employees expect their job to change significantly in 2026 due to technology
- 47% concerned technology will make their role obsolete by 2030
- 93% of executives say they must factor AI sovereignty into their 2026 strategy
- Quantum-ready organizations are 3x more likely to belong to multiple ecosystems than the least ready

## Data Points

- Survey: 1,000+ C-suite executives (via Phronesis) + 8,500 global consumers and employees (via Suzy)
- 96% of executives say the highest-stakes decisions they made in 2025 turned out to be the right ones
- Only 1% of organizations describe their AI rollouts as "mature" (McKinsey State of AI cited)
