## Summary

EFG International's Outlook 2026 (authored by CIO Moz Afzal) identifies 10 themes organized into three clusters: global economic/policy trends; innovation and change; and market opportunities. The framing: "several new and rapidly evolving challenges are emerging alongside long-standing themes." Three macro forces dominate the outlook — geopolitics (multi-polar rivalry with unpredictable volatility), US exceptionalism (will it persist?), and technological advancement (AI investment returns and financing).

The 10 themes span US growth leadership, interest rate inflection points in advanced vs. emerging economies, bond market fiscal risk ("shark-infested waters"), geopolitical new alliances, Trump's "3 Ds" (DOGE/Deregulation/Drugs), AI race opportunities, EU revival via restructuring, emerging market upside, private market liquidity, and IPO/M&A cycle recovery across sectors.

The "Trump's 3 Ds" theme is particularly notable as a structured investment framing: government efficiency (DOGE), deregulation, and drug pricing reform are treated as three distinct policy vectors each creating investable opportunities and disruption.

## Key Claims

- U.S. expected to be the fastest-growing advanced economy in 2026 — structural + cyclical factors support
- Multi-polar world is accelerating: geopolitical news flow will remain volatile and unpredictable
- AI financing and return-on-investment is the central technology question for 2026
- Bond markets face "shark-infested waters" — fiscal sustainability pressure from bond vigilantes
- Trump's 3 Ds (DOGE, Deregulation, Drugs) each create distinct investment themes
- EU revival underway: restructuring and reform creating long-term investment opportunity
- Emerging markets have "wind in their sails" despite US tariff headwinds
- Private markets: unlocking liquidity and diversification opportunities (IPO/M&A cycle improving)
- Multiple sectors set to benefit from IPO and M&A activity rebound
- China remains dominant emerging economy despite structural headwinds
- Interest rates approaching "trough" in advanced economies — higher rates returning as new normal

## Contradictions / Tensions

- EFG is simultaneously bullish on U.S. exceptionalism AND bullish on EU revival — historically these have been in tension. In 2026, EFG argues both can be true simultaneously, which is unusual.
- DOGE-driven government efficiency and drug pricing reform are treated as investment opportunities. The same policies that create market disruption (reducing pharmaceutical profits, cutting government contracts) are being framed as alpha-generating events for investors — a reframing that may not hold if disruption exceeds expected scope.
