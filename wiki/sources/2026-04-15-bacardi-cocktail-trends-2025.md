## Summary
Produced by Bacardi Limited with The Future Laboratory, this 29-page report addresses beverages brands navigating declining spirits volumes (headwinds in 2023–2024) and shifting consumer mindsets toward mindfulness, discernment, and "premiumization with purpose." The report forecasts the drinks industry moving toward "curators and collectors of experiences" rather than "consumers of products." Five 2025 cocktail trends are mapped.

## Key Claims
- Global beverage alcohol expected to begin recovery in 2025 after declines in 2023 and continued challenges in 2024 (IWSR)
- **Consumer shift**: From being consumers of products to "curators and collectors of experiences"
- **Five 2025 Cocktail Trends:**
  1. **Premium Fans**: Consumers seeking fewer, better drinks — quality over quantity; premiumization through education and craft
  2. **In-the-Know Imbibing**: Informed, culturally connected drinking; cocktail menus as cultural signals; bartender as curator-educator
  3. **New Cocktail Frontiers**: Novel ingredients, non-traditional spirits, global flavor influences entering mainstream cocktail culture
  4. **Culinary Connoisseurs**: The convergence of food and cocktail culture; chef-bartender collaboration; drinking as multi-sensory dining
  5. **The Future Spirit**: Alternative positioning for spirits brands in a world of moderation, non-alcoholic options, and wellness culture
- Mindfulness and discernment culture are driving consumers to slower, more intentional alcohol consumption
- Longer-term premiumization is enabled by shifting mindsets — "enabling" rather than "selling"

## Data Points
- Published: Bacardi Limited × The Future Laboratory, 2025; 29 pages
- IWSR: global beverage alcohol to begin recovery in 2025 after 2023–2024 declines
- Spirits category headwinds include competition from RTDs, cannabis beverages, non-alcoholic alternatives
