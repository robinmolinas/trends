## Summary

Accenture's annual Life Trends report (published 2025/2026) identifies five macro trends shaping consumer and employee behavior through the lens of the **hero's journey** — the idea that people across the world are being pulled across thresholds they didn't choose, confronting questions of values and meaning as change accelerates. The framing is deliberately humanistic: people are not passive passengers in a tech-driven world but protagonists writing their own stories.

The report is grounded in substantial primary research: 25,284 consumer survey respondents across 23 countries, 800 C-suite executives across 7 countries, 114 qualitative video interviews in 10 countries, and netnographic analysis of 700M+ online mentions. Workshops were run across 40+ design studios globally (March–April 2025).

The five trends form a coherent picture: as the world destabilizes (Trend 1), AI reshapes personal life bottom-up (Trend 2) and enterprise life top-down (Trend 3); meanwhile people physically and culturally retreat to play and presence (Trend 4); and an entire demographic — the 40+ generation — is rewriting what vitality and productivity mean (Trend 5).

## Key Claims

- 46% of people say their desire for stability has grown stronger; 28% say fatigue and exhaustion have intensified in the past year.
- Over 45% of people are "hooked on the constant cliffhangers of change."
- Trust in traditional journalism has collapsed: only 30% believe journalists don't intentionally mislead (down from 41% four years ago).
- 56% of people report negative emotions: loneliness, anxiety, worry, frustration, fatigue.
- AI adoption is "bottom-up" and personal — people are weaving AI into their own lives faster than brands are directing them to.
- 85% of C-suite leaders planned to increase AI investments in 2025 vs. 2024.
- Only 28% of workforce believe their leaders understand employee AI concerns "moderately well."
- 60% of employees say their leaders provide only moderate guidance on working with AI.
- 40% of people received "workslop" (AI-generated low-quality content masquerading as real work) in the past month.
- 421 million+ online mentions of fear around AI in the past year.
- 48% of people aged 18–24 worry AI will take their job or reduce opportunities.
- 77% of people love moments of spontaneous fun that serve no practical purpose; 71% say the best fun brings them closer to others.
- 29% of people have deliberately avoided mainstream social media for a period.
- 73% prefer authentic experiences (even imperfect ones) over highly curated events.
- The 60+ population has tripled since 1950; will reach 2.1 billion by 2050.
- Baby boomers set to pass on $53 trillion to children by 2045.
- Founders over 45 now account for 25–33% of all new entrepreneurial activity.
- Aesthetic procedures increased 40% between 2021–2023, reaching 34.9M procedures globally in 2023.
- US obesity rates may have peaked in 2020 and are now declining, driven by GLP-1 drugs.
- 28% of 55+ say they feel physically stronger than 3–5 years ago.

## Data Points

- Survey: 25,284 consumers across 23 countries; 800 executives across 7 countries; July 2025
- 65% of executives have set up permanent "war rooms" for economic/political response
- 46% of C-suite executives have shortened their strategic planning horizons
- 82% of executives say it's important to spot subtle offline cultural shifts — yet half rely primarily on AI/predictive insights from large digital datasets
- 72% of consumers expect brands to participate in small, local, community events
- 64% expect brands to put less emphasis on perfection and more on imperfection
- In China: 57% started/increased savoring-the-moment activities; 86% started/increased health-improving activities
- GLP-1 drugs linked to reduced risk of 42 conditions, from dementia to addiction
- US testosterone replacement prescriptions rose from 7.3M (2019) to 11M+ (2024)
- 19% of ages 45–54 and 15% of 55+ track health via wearables

## Contradictions / Tensions

- The report notes a tension between **enterprise AI adoption** (top-down, productivity-focused) and **personal AI use** (bottom-up, exploratory). These are running on parallel tracks and may diverge further.
- **Stability vs. change**: People crave stability but are simultaneously drawn to constant information. 46% want stability but 45% are hooked on change. Both are true simultaneously.
- **Digital flight vs. digital tool dependence**: People are retreating from social media into physical spaces while also using AI tools more than ever. The retreat is selective.
- **Democratization lag**: Longevity-enhancing therapies (GLP-1, HRT, CRISPR) offer enormous potential but affluent/urban populations benefit first, widening health equity gaps.
- **Youth vs. midlife energy**: Report notes older generations gaining cultural/economic momentum while youth faces mounting disadvantages (housing, jobs, AI disruption). Potential for generational tension not fully resolved.
