## Summary

Euromonitor International's annual Global Consumer Trends report (based on multiple proprietary surveys including Voice of the Consumer with 40,000+ respondents across 40+ markets) identifies four behavioral shifts for 2026: Comfort Zone, Fiercely Unfiltered, Rewired Wellness, and Next Asian Wave.

The overarching frame is "navigating contrast" — consumers are simultaneously retreating into comfort and safety while also expressing unapologetic individuality; embracing science-grade wellness tools while rejecting optimization pressure; and gravitating toward Asian aesthetics and culture as a global creative force. Two in three consumers seek to simplify their lives, but nearly half want to be distinct from others. These are not contradictory signals — they reflect the fragmentation of a monolithic consumer into overlapping micro-segments with radically different needs.

The Rewired Wellness trend is particularly data-dense: GLP-1 adoption among weight-loss consumers grew from 6% (2024) to 9% (2025); three in four consumers now track health with a device or app; and 49% would pay a 10%+ premium for beauty products with a scientific formulation. Precision health is no longer aspirational — it is mainstream consumer expectation.

## Key Claims

- 58% of consumers experience moderate to extreme stress daily — the "stress gap" (high levels, low relief)
- 2 in 5 consumers feel under constant pressure to get things done
- Two-thirds of consumers look for ways to simplify their lives
- 3 in 7 consumers "like to enjoy life and don't worry about planning for the future"
- 47% of consumers like to be distinct from others; 50% want products uniquely tailored to them
- 65% of consumers say their identity is accepted by society
- Only 15% associate social acceptance with their definition of beauty
- GLP-1 use among weight-loss consumers: 6% (2024) → 9% (2025)
- 3 in 4 consumers track health with a device or app
- 35% are looking for new prevention or treatment methods because current approaches aren't working
- 49% would pay 10%+ more for beauty products with a scientific formulation
- Consumer belief they can make a difference through choices fell from 56% (2024) to 51% (2025)
- 44% believe climate change will impact their lives more in the next five years
- Global legal recreational cannabis sales projected at ~$40B in 2026; 61% from adult-use products

## Data Points

- Survey base: Voice of the Consumer Lifestyles Survey, January–February 2025, n=40,337
- Health and Nutrition Survey: February 2025, n=21,207
- Beauty Survey: June–July 2025, n=20,206
- Air conditioners: global sales expected to surpass 189M units in 2026
- FMCG natural products: $377.2B in global sales in 2024; 8,000+ new products launched with natural claim (Sep 2024–Aug 2025)
- Botanicals: 2M tonnes used as ingredient across CPG categories globally in 2025
- Scented candles: global sales expected to surpass $3B in 2026
- Cannabis: global market projected at ~$40B in 2026

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Comfort Zone (retreat, simplify, calm) directly coexists with Fiercely Unfiltered (bold expression, nonconformity, doom spending) — different consumer segments or different contexts for the same consumer
- Rewired Wellness (high-tech, clinical precision) contradicts wellness fatigue signals from other sources; Euromonitor sees premium science-wellness as mainstream, not backlash-prone
- Declining sustainability agency (51% → 56% feel they can make a difference) — consumers adapting to climate rather than preventing it (pragmatic adaptation over activism)
