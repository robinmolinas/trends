## Summary
Accenture's Consumer Pulse Research 2025 (18,000 consumers across 14 countries) documents the structural shift in consumer-brand relationships as generative AI becomes a trusted "friend" rather than a mere tool. The report frames AI's role in three ascending stages: trusted guide (LLMs as the new influencers), loyal companion (AI fostering deeper personalized relationships), and second self (agentic AI making autonomous purchases on consumers' behalf).

The central business implication is stark: brands that fail to shape their presence within LLM ecosystems risk being misrepresented or excluded entirely from the AI-mediated consideration set. At the same time, consumer uncertainty (54% see it as the new normal, doubled in a year) means brands competing on experience and authentic connection will win over those competing on price alone.

## Key Claims
- 72% of consumers are regular gen AI users
- 36% of active gen AI users consider the technology a "good friend"
- 9% of consumers now rank gen AI as their single most trusted source for purchase recommendations (fastest growing in past year)
- 54% of consumers see uncertainty as the new normal — doubled in one year
- Gen AI is now active users' #2 purchase recommendation source after physical stores (overtaking social media, search, friends, and brand websites)
- 94% of active gen AI users have or would consider asking it for help with personal development; 87% for social and relationship advice
- 75% of consumers are open to using a trusted AI-powered personal shopper that understands their needs (agentic purchasing)
- 34% of consumers would switch from a preferred brand to one with better AI-enabled personalization
- Proactive AI (anticipating needs vs. just responding) makes brands 19% more likely to be preferred
- Consumers distrust AI-generated content lacking authenticity (41%) and personability (45%)
- Loyalty program members are 1.6× more likely to be retained through AI-enhanced engagement
- SEO must evolve to GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) as LLMs intermediate search

## Data Points
- Sample: 18,000 consumers in 14 countries, 2025
- Physical stores still #1 purchase recommendation source (19%) — gen AI at 18% already second
- Gen AI: 18% of active users' preferred rec source; social media 15%; online marketplace 15%; friends/family 13%; search engine 11%; brand website 10%

- The report argues AI can solve the trust gap, but also notes consumers distrust AI-generated content lacking authenticity (41%) and personability (45%) — the "authentic AI" paradox
- Agentic purchasing promises frictionless commerce but threatens to commoditize brands into interchangeable price/spec comparisons
