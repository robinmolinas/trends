## Summary

PwC Luxembourg's venture capital fundraising series note (authored by Assurance Partner Brieuc Malherbe and Tax Partner Marc Meurant) provides a focused analysis of the European VC fundraising cycle. The core thesis: after three consecutive years of declining VC fundraising, Preqin predicts a comeback by 2026 — with Europe positioned to be a key driver of global VC recovery, even as the current environment remains challenging.

The structural case for European VC: AUM reached nearly €250B in 2024 (+8% YoY); projected CAGR of 10.7% through 2030 (second only to North America's 11.45%); European VC funds have even outperformed North American peers on a 10-year return horizon. The headwinds are real: 2025 is projected to be the third consecutive year of declining VC fundraising in Europe (total capital raised forecast at less than half of 2022 peak). Low distribution rates, shrinking pool of active investors, and capital concentration at large GPs are the proximate causes.

The enabling conditions for recovery are improving: US election resolution, easing interest rates in US and Europe, rebounding institutional appetite (31% of GPs expect institutional investors to increase VC commitments vs. 23% expecting cuts — reversal of 2022–2023 trend). AI is flagged as the expected catalyst for VC's capitalization on innovation momentum.

## Key Claims

- European VC AUM: ~€250B in 2024, up 8% YoY (Preqin)
- European VC projected CAGR: 10.7% through 2030 (second to North America at 11.45%)
- European VC funds outperformed North American peers on a 10-year horizon
- 2025: third consecutive year of declining VC fundraising in Europe; total capital at <50% of 2022 peak
- Key headwinds: low distribution rates, shrinking LP base, capital concentration at large GPs
- Recovery signals: 31% of GPs expect increased institutional commitments vs. 23% expecting cuts (reversal)
- AI expected to catalyze VC industry's capitalization on global innovation momentum
- European fundraising environment is fragmented by diverse legal, regulatory, tax and accounting frameworks
- VC adoption of AI is "finally expected to capitalise on global momentum" in coming years
