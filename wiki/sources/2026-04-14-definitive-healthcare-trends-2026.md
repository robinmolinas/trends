## Summary

Definitive Healthcare's annual healthcare trends forecast (written by senior content writers using Definitive HC's proprietary healthcare analytics platform) identifies seven trends that will meaningfully reshape the US healthcare market in 2026. The seven: (1) AI changing online search behavior and healthcare marketing; (2) aging in place driving home-based care innovation; (3) new PBM rules testing market access strategies; (4) cybersecurity breaches emphasizing secure tech; (5) rising expenses driving cost-containment demand; (6) emerging therapies transforming cancer care; (7) healthcare policy shifts reshaping care business.

The AI search disruption trend is particularly detailed: 61% of American adults used AI in the first half of 2025 (Menlo Ventures), with 58% using gen AI for product/service recommendations (up from 25% in 2023). Google AI Overviews are reducing click-through to traditional websites; healthcare marketers must adapt to GEO (generative engine optimization) alongside traditional SEO. The aging-in-place trend is driven by the surge in adults 65+ (56M in 2020 → 72M projected by 2030) colliding with collapse in nursing home capacity (nearly 63,000 fewer nursing home beds since 2020).

## Key Claims

- 61% of American adults used AI in the first half of 2025; nearly 1 in 5 uses it daily (Menlo Ventures)
- 58% of consumers use gen AI for product/service recommendations in 2025 (up from 25% in 2023, Capgemini)
- 70%+ of consumers want AI integrated into their purchasing experience
- Millennials are AI power users: 24% use AI daily; Gen Z 21%; Gen X 19%; Boomers ~10%
- Healthcare marketers must adapt to GEO — LLMs prioritize logically structured, semantically clear, ungated content
- 75% of US adults age 65+ strongly prefer to age in place (live independently at home)
- US adults 65+: 56M in 2020 → 72M by 2030 → 80M+ by 2040
- 93% of adults age 65+ live with at least one chronic condition; 79% with two or more
- Nearly 63,000 fewer nursing home beds available since 2020
- New skilled nursing facilities opening in 2024: 7 (down from 73 in 2020) — near-total collapse
- 20% of nursing homes have downsized due to labor shortages
- AgeTech is one of the fastest-growing areas in healthcare innovation
- PBM (pharmacy benefit manager) reform rules are testing market access strategies for drug manufacturers
- Cybersecurity breaches in healthcare continue to escalate — secure health tech is a competitive differentiator

## Data Points

- AI adoption: 61% of US adults used AI in H1 2025; 24% Millennials daily (Menlo Ventures)
- Gen AI for recommendations: 58% in 2025 vs 25% in 2023 (Capgemini Research Institute)
- Nursing home beds lost: ~63,000 since 2020; new openings: 7 in 2024 vs 73 in 2020 (AHCA)
- US senior population: 56M (2020) → 72M (2030) → 82M (2050)
- Chronic conditions: 93% of 65+ with at least one; 79% with two or more (National Council on Aging)

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Healthcare AI accuracy is unresolved: Google AI Overviews hallucinate ~1 in 5 searches, yet 61% of US adults are already using AI for healthcare queries. The gap between adoption speed and accuracy creates a structural patient safety risk.
- PBM reform and policy uncertainty create market access instability even as investment in new therapies (cancer, GLP-1) accelerates — innovation and access moving in opposite directions.
