## Summary
McKinsey's September 2024 beauty industry analysis updates their inaugural 2023 state of beauty report with fresh performance data and forecasts through 2028. The global beauty market reached $446 billion in 2023, growing 10 percent — but this headline conceals a critical structural weakness: price increases drove virtually all of the gain while global volume grew only 2 percent. As inflation cools, the era of "de facto price-fuelled growth" is over, forcing beauty brands to pursue genuine differentiation, community, and speed-to-market.

The forecast to 2028 is optimistic but tiered: the sector is expected to reach $590 billion at a 6 percent annual CAGR. Growth dynamics vary sharply by region (Middle East/Africa fastest at 10% CAGR), category (fragrance leads at 7%), and price tier (luxury 9% vs. mass 5%). Asia-Pacific excluding China and Australia remains the largest region and an opportunity for volume expansion. China's 2023 growth was notably weak at 3% and largely price-driven, with recovery expected from Gen Z/millennials and a "lipstick effect" in 2024–2028.

Specialty beauty retail emerged as the channel bright spot — growing 14% in 2023 vs. e-commerce's 8% — driven by younger consumers' preference for "open sell" discovery, in-store services, and social media-bridging experiences. Best-in-class retailers cultivate community ecosystems that extend beyond the in-store visit. E-commerce remains the largest single channel globally but grew at its slowest rate.

The strategic implications are clear: differentiation, agility, AI/gen AI for speed-to-market, and community-building will separate winners from the rest. The luxury segment, while only 4% of the market, is growing the fastest (13%) and is not yet saturated.

## Key Claims
- Global beauty market: $446B in 2023 (up 10% from 2022), forecast to $590B by 2028 at 6% CAGR
- 2023 growth was predominantly price-driven: global volume grew only 2%; US volume +1%
- Fragrance outperformed all categories at 14% growth in 2023, expected to grow 7% annually through 2028
- Luxury beauty grew 13% in 2023 and is forecast at 9% CAGR through 2028 — fastest tier, not yet saturated
- Specialty beauty retail grew 14% in 2023 (vs. e-commerce 8%), emerging as primary discovery/experience hub
- China's beauty market grew only 3% in 2023 — entirely price-driven; volume was flat; consumer confidence low
- Middle East and Africa grew 18% in 2023; expected 10% CAGR through 2028 (highest regionally)
- "Barriers to entry may be coming down, but barriers to scale are only going up" — beauty executive
- Price-fuelled growth era is ending; brands must create real value through differentiation and community

## Data Points
- $446B global beauty retail sales 2023 (Euromonitor/Generation Research/McKinsey)
- $590B forecast for 2028: 6% CAGR globally
- Volume growth breakdown 2023: Global +2%, North America +1%, China +0%
- Regional CAGR 2023–2028: Middle East/Africa 10%, Fragrance category 7%, Luxury tier 9%, Mass 5%
- Skin care: 44% of market, grew 6% in 2023, 6% CAGR expected through 2028
- Makeup: recovered to surpass 2019 levels for first time in 2023
- Specialty retail: +14% in 2023; e-commerce +8%
- Mass tier: 48% of industry value in 2023; up to 20% of US/European consumers traded down to cheaper brands
- Top 3% of MyTheresa clients account for 30% of sales (cited as Vogue Business)

- The $590B by 2028 forecast is directionally consistent with the earlier $580B by 2027 from the State of Fashion Beauty 2023 report, but the timeline shifted one year later — suggesting the 2023 growth was slightly slower than expected at the volume level
- China growth divergence is stark: the market that drove global luxury aspirations is underperforming; whether lipstick effect or Gen Z spending accelerates recovery remains uncertain
- E-commerce is the largest single channel but growing slowest — platform saturation + return to in-store discovery creates channel strategy complexity
