## Summary

State Street Investment Management's Global Market Outlook 2026 ("Forward with Focus") takes a stance of cautious optimism, framing 2026 as "the year of delayed policy impact." The core thesis: the major policy disruptions of 2025 (tariffs, immigration, OBBBA stimulus, Germany's €500B fiscal reset) are still passing through their transmission channels — full impact arrives in 2026, not 2025. The macro environment supports continued growth but demands selective, focus-driven investment.

The AI arms race is a central investment theme: the U.S. and China currently lead, but the Middle East is carving out a strategic position through chip sourcing, power infrastructure, and talent development. Germany and Japan have committed to AI investment as part of broader fiscal and strategic modernization. However, 2026 is still "too soon" to see AI's productivity impact in macro data — it remains a forward promise, not yet a measured reality.

Trade policy complexity remains live: USMCA renegotiation with Mexico/Canada and deals with India, Brazil, Taiwan, and Switzerland are all in motion. The Middle East is positioning as an AI infrastructure hub, complicating the US-China duopoly frame. Currency risk for non-US investors is flagged as significant — a weaker USD could offset US equity gains.

## Key Claims

- 2026 is "the year of delayed policy impact" — tariff, immigration, and fiscal effects still transmitting
- Fed: up to 3 rate cuts expected in 2026; BoE expected to catch up on easing; ECB to pause near-term
- Bank of Japan: cautiously hiking (opposite direction from other central banks)
- Germany's €500B defense/infrastructure fiscal package expected to lift European earnings in 2026
- US, China, and Middle East are primary AI arms-race actors — Middle East entering as third player
- AI productivity impact: "too soon" to see in macro data in 2026 — it's a wave of investment, not yet output
- AI investment visible as "global wave of data processing capacity" build-out
- Japan positioning as "world's most AI-friendly country for development and utilization" under PM Takaichi
- Trade deals in progress: US-UK, US-EU, US-Japan concluded; US-India closing; US-China bilateral progress post Trump-Xi meeting
- Non-US investors face USD weakness risk on US equity holdings — selective hedging recommended
- Gold topped $4,000/oz in October 2025 — signal of uncertainty premium

## Contradictions / Tensions

- State Street describes AI's productivity impact as unmeasurable in macro terms for 2026, yet all equity strategy is being oriented around AI as the dominant theme. This creates a structural investment-vs-evidence tension: capital is flowing into AI's promise, not yet its demonstrated productivity.
- The simultaneous easing (US, EU, UK) and tightening (Japan) of central banks marks the most divergent global monetary regime in decades. This complicates currency hedging and EM positioning simultaneously.
