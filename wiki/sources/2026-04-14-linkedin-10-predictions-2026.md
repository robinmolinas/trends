## Summary

LinkedIn News Europe's annual "Big Ideas" predictions piece (published December 16, 2025) compiles 10 bold predictions from LinkedIn editors, industry experts, and prominent executives including Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon. The predictions span autonomous transportation, the loneliness epidemic, resilient globalization, smart pills, smartphone bans for children, biometrics in the workplace, European stablecoins, luxury + celebrity convergence, GEO replacing SEO, and event tourism.

The loneliness prediction is particularly detailed and sourced: 82% of UK adults have experienced loneliness; nearly 10% of EU residents have no close friends (OECD). AI companionship is framed as a "band-aid at best, active contributor at worst" — and the prediction is that investments in genuine human connection will prove the most effective response. The globalization prediction (from David Solomon, Goldman Sachs CEO) argues for "resilient globalization" — trade is adapting, not collapsing. The stablecoin prediction is highly specific: a consortium of 9 European banks (including ING and UniCredit) plan to launch a euro-backed stablecoin in H2 2026.

## Key Claims

- Autonomous vehicles: Waymo London debut pending regulatory approval; Uber/Wavy robotaxi pilot in UK; VW self-driving minivans (Moia) in Hamburg
- 82% of UK adults have experienced loneliness; nearly 10% of EU residents have no close friends (OECD)
- AI companionship is "a band-aid at best, an active contributor at worst" to the loneliness crisis
- David Solomon (Goldman Sachs): "resilient globalization" — trade adapting, not collapsing; "friend-shoring" + on-shoring creating new supply chain logic
- Smart pills: global market now $5B, expected to surpass $15B by 2033
- UK's £51B annual mental health costs — a driver of biometric workplace wellness adoption
- Smartphone ban movement going mainstream: Australia under-16 social media ban; Denmark school ban 2026–27; 20 US states with classroom phone bans
- European stablecoin: 9-bank consortium (ING, UniCredit, others) launching euro-backed stablecoin H2 2026
- Stablecoin market: $280B (98% dollar-pegged); growing 75% YoY (Bank of Italy)
- GEO: "the SEO industry is being pulled reluctantly into the GEO era" — concern about "dead internet" if ads don't appear in chatbots
- Event tourism market: $1.7T currently, projected to soar to $2.6T by 2035
- Sports tourism: 10% of global tourism spending
- Gen Z travellers: 62% plan to spend more on experiences than possessions in next 12 months
- Music tourists spent a record £10B in the UK in 2024
- Luxury brands using celebrity "all-star squads" — Burberry, Prada, Christian Louboutin examples

## Contradictions / Tensions

- The loneliness prediction names AI companionship as potentially harmful while the biometrics/wearables prediction sees tech-mediated wellness as transformative. The same "tech-vs.-human" tension plays out in two different predictions without resolution.
- Resilient globalization (Solomon's prediction) directly counters the deglobalization/fragmentation narrative prominent in BCG Geopolitics and investment outlooks. Solomon's case is more optimistic than most other sources — worth watching as a contrarian signal.
