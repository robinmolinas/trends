## Summary

WGSN's Future Consumer 2026 forecast (produced using their proprietary STEPIC methodology — Society, Technology, Environment, Politics, Industry, Creativity — combined with TrendCurve+ predictive analytics from social media, search, e-commerce, and catwalk data) identifies four consumer profiles driving change in 2026. This executive summary version provides full detail on one profile (The Gleamers) while the remaining three (The Impartialists, The Autonomists, The Synergists) are gated for subscribers.

The four profiles collectively represent a fragmentation of consumer motivation: Impartialists seek facts over fiction; Autonomists set their own rules and reject societal norms; Gleamers abandon hustle culture and corporate optimization in favor of small wins and community; Synergists advocate for human-tech symbiosis and cultural contraflows.

The Gleamers profile is the most detailed and actionable: burnout-driven consumers rejecting traditional milestones in favor of "minorstones" (small, personal wins). They're pioneering urban flight to suburban/rural locations, embracing "found families" (chosen kin), and seeking brands that celebrate care, community, and achievable progress. Their evolution from WGSN's prior profiles shows a clear arc: 2024 Time Keepers (daily rituals) → 2025 Memory Makers (presence over perfection) → 2026 Gleamers (scaling back societal expectations to scale up daily joy).

## Key Claims

- Four 2026 consumer profiles: The Impartialists, The Autonomists, The Gleamers, The Synergists
- The Gleamers reject "corporate optimisation" and redefine success on a personal scale — "minorstones" over milestones
- 42% of workers in Japan, Australia, US, UK, France, Germany say they are burned out (Future Forum Pulse)
- 49% of consumers would be more likely to purchase from a brand that brings them a sense of joy (VML)
- +62% YoY increase in social media buzz around ethical chocolate — Gleamers' ethical consumption signal
- Art and craft market projected at $74.5B in 2031 (from $44.1B in 2021) — driven by therapeutic hobbies trend
- 12B working days lost globally to depression and anxiety annually (WHO)
- Gleamers are pioneering urban-to-rural migration (WGSN has tracked since 2021)
- The Impartialists seek "facts over fiction" — radical transparency over polished storytelling
- The Synergists advocate for "cultural contraflows and human-tech symbiosis"
- WGSN uses STEPIC (Society, Technology, Environment, Politics, Industry, Creativity) methodology
