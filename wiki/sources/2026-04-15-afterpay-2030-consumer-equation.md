## Summary
Produced by The Future Laboratory for Afterpay (Block's BNPL service), this 21-page report forecasts the consumer retail landscape to 2030. It argues that brands must go "beyond transaction to inspire connection" with next-gen consumers, and that technology must prove genuine utility ("tech-tility") rather than novelty. Three core tensions define the consumer equation of 2030: (1) digital vs. physical spheres fusing, (2) algorithmic sameness vs. authentic self-expression demand, and (3) BNPL/fintech reshaping access to commerce.

## Key Claims
- **Tech-tility**: Technology is transitioning from a consumer good to infrastructure akin to a human right. PwC forecasts AI contributing to AUS$23.8 trillion by 2030, with 45% of gains from product enhancements. 71% of organizations already using AI (Microsoft data).
- **Ephemeral Culture**: Algorithmic overload is flattening culture into sameness — 73% of Australian Gen Z feel pressure to keep up with trending topics (Snapchat); 66% are fatigued by "new" content that's simply more of the same. Brands that foster rather than hinder authentic self-expression will win.
- **Dazed Media reports 217% increase** in brands partnering with cultural figures to boost influence — culture-first commerce.
- **Next-gen consumers seek depth and connection** beyond transaction — Millennials and Gen Z want to be "part of the brand story" as knowledgeable fans, not passive consumers.
- **Fintech and retail converging**: BNPL services like Afterpay are becoming infrastructure layers enabling new purchase behaviors and democratizing premium access.
