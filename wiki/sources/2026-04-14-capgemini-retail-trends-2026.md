## Summary

Capgemini's 2026 Retail Trends report (featuring frog design/strategy input) identifies five forces reshaping how customers discover, decide, and buy: (1) Moments over Merchandise — experience economy as retail necessity; (2) The Year of the Private Label — private labels going premium; (3) Searchless Retail — GEO replacing SEO; (4) Invisible AI — frictionless customer experiences built behind the scenes; (5) Trust as a Profit Driver — consistency, care, and contextualization driving margins.

The GEO/searchless retail trend is particularly data-rich: one in five of Walmart's referral clicks in August 2025 came from ChatGPT, up 15% from July. 46% of shoppers now make purchases based on AI recommendations; 46% are willing to order products via AI tools (e.g., Perplexity's Shop like a Pro). The private label trend shows affluent adoption: 70% of shoppers earning over $100K now choose their grocery store based on private labels; Millennial and Gen Z shoppers are nearly twice as likely as Boomers to spend more on private labels.

The scrimp-vs.-splurge insight reinforces FMCG Gurus and other sources: 7 in 10 consumers use small indulgences to cope with financial stress (71%) — confirming the dual-track consumer economy.

- Travel and hospitality experiences are the #1 luxury spending category globally — outpacing fashion, cars, beauty
- 70% of shoppers earning >$100K choose their grocery store based on private labels
- Millennials and Gen Z nearly 2x as likely as Boomers to spend more on private labels
- 44% of shoppers buying private-label or low-cost brands over name brands
- 1 in 5 of Walmart's referral clicks in August 2025 came from ChatGPT (+15% from July)
- 46% of shoppers make purchases based on AI recommendations
- 46% willing to order products via AI tools (e.g., Perplexity's Shop like a Pro)
- 7 in 10 consumers use small indulgences to cope with financial stress (71%)
- Success in retail is no longer about keyword search rankings — it's about being "chosen by an algorithm"
- Private labels driving loyalty, traffic, and differentiation — no longer a race to the bottom

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Private label premiumization is happening at the same time as budget pressure on lower-income consumers. The "private label as luxury" trend applies mainly to high-income shoppers; for lower-income consumers, private label is still a cost-reduction strategy. Same product category, opposite motivations.
