## Summary

ITONICS (works with 500+ innovation leaders) draws on 1,000+ cross-industry conversations to identify six shifts separating innovation performers from storytellers in 2026. The core finding: innovation accountability is moving *closer to business units* while visibility, governance, and coordination are being *centralized*. The mandate has shifted from exploration to measurable value creation. Innovation must perform, not just promise.

The "AI pilots lack value" problem is acute: most large organizations now run 20–40 AI experiments but cannot articulate combined strategic impact or prevent duplication. CFOs are tightening portfolio discipline. Fewer than 30% of organizations successfully translate innovation into core business or scalable revenue. Dedicated innovation functions are being absorbed into R&D, IT, and strategy; unified innovation PMOs are emerging as the governance backbone.

## Key Claims

- Most large organizations run 20–40 AI experiments but struggle to show combined strategic impact
- Fewer than 30% of organizations successfully translate innovation into core business/scalable revenue
- Dedicated innovation functions being absorbed into R&D, IT, and strategy — decentralization of accountability, centralization of visibility
- Innovation ROI is the new mandate: "achieve more with less" is the explicit CFO directive
- AI is not a standalone innovation project — it must be embedded in business and product decisions

## Data Points

- 20–40 AI experiments running simultaneously in most large organizations
- <30% successfully translate innovation into scalable revenue
- 1,000+ innovation leader conversations across sectors/geographies
