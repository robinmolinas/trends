## Summary

Insightios' e-commerce trends report (visual slide format) provides a data-dense overview of four "hidden" structural forces reshaping online retail in 2025–2026: shifting product demand toward health/wellness and sustainable niches; AI-powered personalization becoming table stakes; rising digital advertising costs squeezing seller margins; and the DTC-vs-marketplace strategic tension.

The scale context is striking: global online retail sales projected at $6.86 trillion in 2025 (21% of all retail), with 2.77 billion digital shoppers globally (roughly one-third of the world's population). AI adoption in e-commerce has grown 270% since 2019; 80% of companies are using or evaluating AI technologies. The AI-in-e-commerce market is projected at $8.65B in 2025, growing at 14.6% CAGR to ~$17B by 2030.

The advertising cost inflation story is a counterweight to the AI growth narrative: Amazon's average CPC rose ~20% from 2023 to 2024; Walmart marketplace ads saw ~23% CPC jump. Digital advertising has become a structural margin headwind for e-commerce sellers — driving shifts toward owned channels, retail media networks, and AI-driven ad automation.

- Amazon average CPC rose ~20% from 2023 to 2024
- Walmart marketplace ad CPC jumped ~23% YoY
- Only 3% of e-commerce companies report no plans to use AI

- 43% of employees cite lack of AI knowledge/skills as major hurdle
- 66% of global consumers willing to pay more for sustainable products
- Global health and wellness market projected at $1.5T by 2025
- Electronics largest e-commerce category: ~$922.5B in consumer spend in 2024
- Fashion second: ~$760B in online sales

## Contradictions / Tensions

- AI adoption is universal but ROI is uneven: 80% are "using or evaluating" AI, but data silos, skills gaps, and security concerns mean the majority are not yet capturing the 10–12% revenue uplift. Early movers and large platforms capture the AI dividend first.
- Rising ad costs and AI efficiency gains are on a collision course: AI reduces operational costs, but higher CPCs erode those gains on the revenue acquisition side. Net margin benefit is not assured.
