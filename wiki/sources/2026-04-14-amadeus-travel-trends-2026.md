## Summary

Amadeus (global travel technology company) and Globetrender (travel trend forecaster Jenny Southan) co-authored this Travel Trends 2026 report. The framing: "The future of travel is on 'spin mode' — with technology, culture and innovation propelling it into hyper-drive." Five trends are featured: (1) The Pawprint Economy — pets gaining dignity and rights in travel; (2) AI-powered trip planning; (3) long-range narrow-body jets shrinking global distances; (4) pop culture-inspired pilgrimages; (5) hyper-personalized hotel stays.

The Pawprint Economy is the most data-rich trend: global pet industry projected to grow 45% to $500B by 2030; 56% of people globally now own a pet (Mars study, 20,000 people, Oct 2024); 27% of pet parents who traveled with pets on their main vacation in 2025 were doing so for the first time. Travel barriers for pet owners are being systematically dismantled by technology, legislation, and hospitality design.

The overall travel sentiment is highly bullish: Amadeus data confirms travel demand growth with AI becoming an embedded planning tool. "2026 will feel more 'science fiction' than any year that has come before," per Globetrender CEO — pointing to AI, aircraft innovation, and pop culture as accelerating forces.

## Key Claims

- Global pet industry projected to grow 45% to $500B valuation by 2030 (Bloomberg)
- 56% of people globally now own a pet (Mars study, 20,000 people, Oct 2024)
- 47% identify as first-time pet owners — new generation with different human-animal relationship norms
- 27% of pet parents who vacationed with pets in 2025 were doing so for the first time
- 42% cite "travel would've been too stressful for their pet" as primary barrier to pet travel
- Long-range narrow-body jets (like A321XLR) enabling direct routes that were previously impossible
- Pop culture pilgrimages: travel motivated by film, TV, and entertainment franchise connection
- AI is embedding into trip planning as standard (blending machine intelligence with human instinct)
- Hyper-personalized hotel stays: guests designing every detail of their stay

## Data Points

- Global pet industry: $500B by 2030 (Bloomberg); current penetration 56% global ownership
- First-time pet vacationers in 2025: 27% (Shape Insight survey, 2,896 travelers, UK and USA, Oct–Nov 2025)
- Mars study: 20,000 people globally, October 2024

## Contradictions / Tensions

- The Pawprint Economy trend (pets gaining travel rights and dignity) reflects a broader shift in how younger consumers relate to companion animals — but it also reflects declining birth rates and delayed family formation. Pets are a proxy for the delayed and/or replaced human family unit. This deeper socioeconomic signal is not addressed in the report.
