## Summary
The Future Today Strategy Group (FTSG) retired its 19-year-old annual Tech Trends Report to launch a new format: the Convergence Outlook. The central argument is that the future no longer arrives one trend at a time — multiple technological, economic, geopolitical, and social forces are converging in ways that create net new realities greater than the sum of their parts. A "convergence" is defined as system-level, irreversible, power-redistributing, and impossible to understand by tracking individual trends alone.

The report maps 10 convergences organized across five sections: (1) Power Is Physical Again (compute as geographically constrained resource), (2) When Machines Take the Wheel (agentic economies and labor displacement), (3) A World That Watches Back (human augmentation and surveillance capitalism), (4) When Systems Become Alive (synthetic biology and living intelligence), (5) Who We Turn To Now (autonomous care and emotional outsourcing to AI).

The framing thesis borrows from Schumpeter's "creative destruction": organizations that refuse to dismantle what worked yesterday will calcify and become irrelevant as convergences reorganize markets without them.

## Key Claims

**10 Convergences:**
- **Compute Shock**: AI infrastructure is now geographically constrained by electricity and water availability, turning obscure locales into strategic assets. Sovereignty over compute is becoming as important as energy or currency sovereignty. Single AI data centers now draw hundreds of megawatts. Sovereign compute shatters the globally optimized hyperscaler model.
- **Polycompute**: Computing is diversifying beyond centralized cloud — edge, quantum, neuromorphic, and specialized chips create a heterogeneous compute landscape.
- **Agentic Economies & Post-Search Internet**: Human-initiated transactions migrate to machine-executed outcomes. Agent-to-agent commerce at machine speed operates beyond human inspection or institutional oversight. The great competitive struggle ahead is over "delegated authority" — who controls the rails agents run on, sets their default objective functions, and shapes what they can see.
- **The New Labor Equation**: AI rewrites what work means faster than labor markets, education, and policy can adapt.
- **Human Augmentation**: AI, biotech, and sensors extend human capability — raising questions of access, identity, and competitive fairness.
- **The Corporate Panopticon**: Data collection, behavioral prediction, and enterprise surveillance reach unprecedented granularity.
- **Living Intelligence**: AI merges with biological systems, creating programmable living materials and synthetic intelligence.
- **Programmable Biology**: CRISPR, synthetic biology, and biofoundries industrialize biological design at scale.
- **Autonomous Care**: AI agents take over health monitoring, care coordination, and therapeutic delivery.
- **Emotional Outsourcing**: Consumers increasingly delegate emotional labor, companionship, and therapeutic support to AI.

## Data Points
- Single AI data centers now draw hundreds of megawatts, colliding with grid capacity constraints
- Frontier AI workloads double every 6–10 months, pushing compute demand past efficiency gains
- The report draws on ~20 years of continuous foresight research across industries and geographies
- Convergences Landscape categorizes forces into Stable Ground, Pressure Zones, and Fault Lines

## Contradictions / Tensions
- The "creative destruction" framing assumes organizations can self-cannibalize before markets do — but the same Compute Shock convergence suggests infrastructure advantages accrue to large incumbents and nation-states, not agile newcomers
- Emotional Outsourcing and the Loneliness Epidemic are in tension: does AI companionship solve or deepen the connection deficit?
