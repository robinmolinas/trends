## Summary
The Future Laboratory's Luxury Futures 2023 report (sample edition) introduces the "Luxury Recrafted" macrotrend, arguing that luxury brands are evolving from purveyors of exclusivity into cultural leaders who set trends and drive aspirations globally. The sector's challenge: as social media has democratized access to luxury knowledge — Gen Z and millennials see luxury as approachable rather than intimidating — brands must simultaneously serve a highly knowledgeable community of younger aspirants while protecting and deepening relationships with their most valuable, high-net-worth clients.

The report identifies six Trends Rising (nascent trends forecast to grow 1–3 years): Gated Retail (appointment-only experiences for VICs), Knowledge Clubs (connoisseurship as status), Cleaner Carats (ethical fine jewellery), Branded Living (designer lives, not just lifestyles), Heirloom Allure (Gen Z's archive fascination), and Air-scapism (airborne luxury travel). These trends collectively signal luxury's strategic response: deepen exclusivity at the very top (Gated Retail), build community-of-knowing (Knowledge Clubs), and create universe extensions beyond core product (Branded Living).

The Luxury Recrafted macrotrend argues that luxury innovation will come from both behemoths (LVMH, Kering) and independent startups enabling new models — from digital-circular platforms like Syky to resale services like Save Your Wardrobe. The sector needs both kinds of actors to future-proof.

## Key Claims
- Luxury brands are evolving from exclusivity purveyors into cultural leaders setting trends and driving aspirations globally
- Gen Z and younger millennials are "tech- and archive-savvy," seeking community and belonging, not just products
- Gated Retail is luxury's response to "Uneasy Affluence": appointment-only, invitation-only spaces for VICs who drive disproportionate revenue
- Connoisseurship ("Knowledge Clubs") is the new status symbol — belonging to a community of knowing matters as much as having
- Cleaner Carats: Gen Z doesn't view sustainability as a trend but as identity — jewellery sustainability = ethics + circularity + conflict-free sourcing
- Branded Living: car-branded condos signal willingness to expand luxury into full lifestyle categories (homes, not just accessories)
- Heirloom Allure: Gen Z turning to vintage and archive markets, spotlighting once-side-lined secondary luxury goods
- Air-scapism: new airborne luxury cruise experiences cater to growing appetite for adventure-with-exclusivity

## Data Points
- MyTheresa: top 3% of clients account for 30% of sales (source: Vogue Business, cited in report)
- Gucci opened appointment-only salon in Los Angeles in April 2023
- Beauty resale was top-five fastest-growing resale category, projected to grow 126% by 2031 (cited in Map the Future Preso 1 from same period)
- Independent startups (Syky, AOI, Save Your Wardrobe) cited as co-equal innovation actors alongside LVMH/Kering

## Contradictions / Tensions
- Report argues luxury is becoming more approachable/democratic (social media effect) while simultaneously recommending "Gated Retail" — these are in direct tension; resolution is bifurcation within luxury itself (accessible halo + ultra-exclusive core)
- "Luxury Recrafted" as cultural leadership implies brands must generate cultural relevance beyond products — few luxury maisons have successfully made this transition; execution risk is high
- This is a 2023 sample report; the macrotrend framing will need updating against the BCG True Luxury 2024 data on luxury market contraction at the aspirational tier
