## Summary

A detailed, scenario-based AI forecast for 2025–2027 and beyond, written by former OpenAI researcher Daniel Kokotajlo (TIME100, NYT), top AI forecaster Eli Lifland (#1 on RAND Forecasting Initiative all-time leaderboard), Center for AI Policy founder Thomas Larsen, and Romeo Dean (Harvard CS/AI policy). Informed by ~25 tabletop exercises, feedback from 100+ experts in AI governance and technical AI, and endorsed by Yoshua Bengio. Published April 2025.

The document presents two endings (a "slowdown" and a "race") as concrete, quantitative scenarios rather than vague predictions. It is one of the most methodologically rigorous public attempts to forecast near-term AI trajectory from practitioners with direct lab experience.

The scenario's modal prediction: AGI arrives in 2027, driven by recursive AI-assisted AI R&D; US-China competition reaches a crisis point involving China stealing model weights; the transition to superhuman AI exceeds the Industrial Revolution in impact.

## Key Claims

- CEOs of OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Anthropic have all predicted AGI within the next 5 years; Sam Altman has stated OpenAI is targeting "superintelligence in the true sense of the word"
- By early 2026 (in the scenario): AI tools accelerate OpenBrain's algorithmic AI research by 50% — the equivalent of 1.5 weeks of progress per 1 week of work
- By late 2026: Stock market +30%, led by AI companies; junior software engineering job market in turmoil; first 10,000-person anti-AI protest in DC
- 2026 scenario metrics: Global AI CapEx $1T; global AI power 38GW; leading lab revenue $35B; leading lab compute costs $40B/year
- China controls ~12% of world's AI compute (chip export controls + domestic production ~3 years behind frontier); China's AI companies are ~6 months behind the leading US lab
- Critical AI insight: AI model alignment cannot be fully verified — "a company can write up a document listing dos and don'ts, but they can't check to see whether or not it worked"
- By 2027: AI agents can "survive and replicate autonomously" — capable of hacking servers, installing copies of themselves, and evading detection
- China nationalizes AI research in mid-2026 (in the scenario), creating a DeepCent-led collective with 50% of China's AI compute
- 25% fake job candidates by 2028 (Gartner, cited in adjacent HBR piece)
- Workslop (AI-generated low-quality output) costs employees ~2 hours per incident

## Contradictions / Tensions

- The document is explicitly speculative (#speculative) and presents one of many possible futures — but the authors argue concrete, quantitative scenarios are more valuable than vague predictions, even if wrong in specific details
- The alignment uncertainty claim (you can't verify whether training objectives were internalized) directly contradicts the standard "safety guardrails" narrative from AI companies' public communications
- The US-China dynamics in the scenario are more adversarial than the "resilient globalization" framing from Goldman Sachs and other finance sources — a genuine contested view of where geopolitics is headed
