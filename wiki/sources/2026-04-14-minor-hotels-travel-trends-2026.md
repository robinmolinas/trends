## Summary

Minor Hotels' inaugural travel behaviour survey-based Travel Trends Report 2026 ("Travelling Deeper: A Search for Lasting Connection") provides a hospitality-centric view of how travel motivations are shifting. The central thesis: "2026 will be the year of Connection." Travel is shifting from escapism (checking out of routines) to active meaning-seeking (catalyzing healthier, more fulfilling lives).

The data from Minor Hotels' inaugural travel behaviour survey paints a strongly bullish picture: 94% of respondents expect to travel as much or more in the coming year; one-third plan more trips than in 2025; 94% plan to spend the same or more; nearly half (47%) intend to increase their travel budgets. Luxury travellers are nearly twice as likely to increase travel frequency (61% vs. 34% overall).

Authentic cultural connection is the leading decision driver: 83% consider local cultural experiences important in destination choice; culinary exploration is the #1 gateway to connection (85%); 76% have revisited a destination because of an emotional connection formed there; 79% prefer independent exploration over guided experiences. Sustainability is increasingly a selection criterion: 47% say a hotel's sustainability credentials influence accommodation choice.

## Key Claims

- 94% of travellers expect to travel as much or more in 2026
- One-third plan more trips in 2026 than in 2025
- 94% plan to spend the same or more on travel; 47% intend to increase budgets
- Luxury travellers nearly twice as likely to increase trip frequency (61% vs. 34% overall)
- 83% consider local cultural experiences important in destination choice
- Culinary exploration is the #1 gateway to connection (85%)
- 71% highlight discovering historic architecture as important; 65% enjoying nature
- 79% prefer independent exploration over guided experiences
- 76% have revisited a destination for emotional connection reasons
- 47% say a hotel's sustainability credentials influence accommodation choice
- Travel is shifting from "escapism" to "catalyst for healthier, more fulfilling lives"
- MICE (meetings, incentives, conferences, events) represents over a quarter of Minor Hotels' global revenue

## Contradictions / Tensions

- 94% travel optimism from a single hotel chain's customer survey is a self-selecting sample of existing hospitality consumers — not representative of general population. The bullishness should be read as existing traveler intent, not market-wide prediction.
- The "deeper connection" framing (WGSN Gleamers archetype, Minor Hotels frame) is in tension with the rising solo travel trend and individualization signals elsewhere. Connection is sought but on highly personal, self-directed terms.
