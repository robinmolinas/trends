## Summary
Commissioned by G42 (Abu Dhabi-based AI company), The Future Laboratory maps how AI will transform professional sport across five domains: Strategy & Tactics, Talent, Engagement, Health & Performance, and Design. The central argument is that sport will always be human at its core, but AI-driven capabilities can augment expert teams, improve athletic outcomes, enable global talent discovery, and create more immersive fan experiences.

## Key Claims
- **AI + Strategy & Tactics**: Real-time game analysis, opponent modeling, training optimization, in-game decision support for coaches. AI reduces hours of video analysis to seconds.
- **AI + Talent**: Global talent scouting democratized by vision AI — potential stars in underserved markets become discoverable without expensive physical scouting networks. Predictive athleticism modeling from early age.
- **AI + Engagement**: Fan experiences personalized at scale — broadcast personalization, AR/VR immersion, real-time stats overlays, AI-generated commentary, and interactive prediction markets.
- **AI + Health & Performance**: Biometric monitoring (wearables + vision AI), injury prediction, personalized recovery protocols, sleep and nutrition optimization. AI enables individual athlete optimization previously only available to elite programs.
- **AI + Design**: AI-generated kit and equipment design, stadium optimization, training facility design tuned to performance data.
- **G42's positioning**: G42 frames AI as "a powerful force for good, with almost unlimited potential to enhance people's abilities and lives" — sport as a domain to demonstrate this

## Data Points

## Contradictions / Tensions
- G42 is a UAE state-backed AI company; the "AI for Good" framing must be read in context of state-linked tech geopolitics
- Global talent democratization via AI could paradoxically increase inequality if only wealthy clubs/programs can afford the systems that discover and develop global talent
- AI-driven fan engagement (personalized broadcast, AR overlays) may fragment shared sporting experiences that depend on collective in-the-moment emotion
