## Summary
The Business of Fashion × McKinsey Beauty special edition (May 2023, 83 pages) provides the structural baseline for the beauty industry through 2027. While published in 2023, it establishes the foundational market architecture that more recent sources build on. Key thesis: beauty demonstrates exceptional resilience vs. other consumer sectors — growing through economic turbulence while other categories struggle — because beauty is increasingly tethered to wellness, identity, and self-care, not discretionary aesthetics.

## Key Claims
- Global beauty industry: **$427B in 2023 → $580B by 2027**, growing at 6% per year
- **Luxury beauty opportunity**: $20B today → $40B by 2027 — a doubling in 4 years
- Geographic concentration risk: US (~$115B by 2027) and China (~$96B by 2027) remain dominant but growth harder; **Middle East and India emerging as new hotspots**
- **Wellness convergence**: Beauty's march into wellness is a structural shift — ingestibles, supplements, functional skincare; beauty now part of the $1.5T global wellness industry
- **Gen Z as force**: Will represent 25% of global population by 2030; already commands social media channels and challenges mainstream beauty definitions; demands brands be "multidimensional" and wellness-integrated
- **Challenger brands at scaling challenge**: Niche disruptors face the "scale imperative" — growing from cult status to commercial scale
- **M&A environment**: Megadeals fewer and farther between post-2023; deal-makers focus on brands that can demonstrate profitability with innovative pipelines, not just growth
- **Fragrance as luxury crossover**: Fragrance is the highest-growth beauty luxury category, crossing into mainstream social currency

## Data Points
- $427B beauty market (2023) → $580B (2027 forecast), 6% CAGR
- Luxury beauty: $20B → $40B by 2027 (+100%)
- Gen Z = 25% of global population by 2030
- China beauty market: ~$96B by 2027; North America: ~$115B by 2027
- Wellness beauty industry embedded in $1.5T global wellness market
- Survey: global consumer shopping behaviors across 6 core beauty markets
- Published: May 2023, BoF + McKinsey

## Contradictions / Tensions
- As a 2023 document, some market size projections (2027) may be altered by post-2023 GLP-1 adoption, AI content disruption, and tariff environment
- "Beauty resilience" thesis has been complicated by 2024–2025 luxury slowdown in China (documented elsewhere) and softening mass market
