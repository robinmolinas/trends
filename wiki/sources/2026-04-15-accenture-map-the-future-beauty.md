## Summary
Two Accenture workshop presentations (apparently prepared for a client engagement) mapping the macro-contextual forces reshaping the future of beauty, and the "future-proof promises" a beauty brand could pursue. Preso 1 functions as an environmental scan across 10 macro categories (Demography, Public Health, Environment, Geopolitics, Economy, Wealth, Education, Infrastructure, Media/Comms) mapped against four "Areas of Study" (Future of Self & Representation, Future of What's Living, Future of Sensoriality & Senses, Future of Empowerment). Preso 2 articulates 8 future-proof beauty promises and a framework for what makes a brand a category leader.

The demographic "hard truths" framing is notable: by 2030, the world will have 2 billion Gen Zers and 2 billion people over 50 simultaneously — making any single-demographic beauty strategy obsolete. Wealth is concentrating (top 1% → 2/3 of global wealth by 2030) while the middle class expands (60% of global population vs. 25% in 2008), driven primarily by China and India. The health reality is stark: NCDs responsible for 70% of global deaths; neurological disease is now the world's #1 disability driver. These signals converge on a "medi-grade" beauty opportunity where products must work clinically, not cosmetically.

Preso 2 defines 8 future-proof beauty promises for winning brands: Singularity (precision for divergent identities), Vitality (post-demographic, life-stage support), Metabolic Power, Responsive/Chameleon adaptation, Synthetic identity editing, Omniconvenience (ambient/automated beauty), Hedonism/letting go, and Collective/community healing. The leadership framework that closes the deck — distilled from Apple, Nike, Tesla, Cartier, Lacoste, Audemars Piguet, McDonald's — emphasizes 10 capabilities: rebel against establishment, fuel consumer ego, offer belonging, care for fan base, ATAWAD daily integration, elevate daily life, design ecosystem, create culture, remix/hybridize, and be "quantic/multitemporal" (playing past, present, future simultaneously).

## Key Claims
- By 2030: 2B Gen Zers AND 2B people 50+; 50% of world spending by 50+ yo; Asia home to 60% of world's 65+
- Top 1% will control ~2/3 of global wealth by 2030; 100 million more rich people added to global population
- 60% of world population middle class by 2030 (vs. 25% in 2008); China + India = 59% of worldwide middle class
- NCDs responsible for 70% of global deaths; neurological disease = #1 cause of disability worldwide
- In Europe by 2030: 1 in 5 people will have suffered skin cancer before age 75
- Home-use beauty devices market: CAGR 25.1% between 2020–2030
- Global wearable technology market: estimated $283B by 2027
- Beauty resale projected to grow 126% by 2031 (top-5 fastest growing resale category)
- Global biomimetic materials market projected to reach $65.9B by 2030
- 68% of world population will live in urban areas by 2050 (up from 55% today)
- Beauty of the future = 8 promises: Singularity, Vitality (post-demographic), Metabolic, Responsive/Chameleon, Synthetic (biohacking), Omniconvenience, Hedonism, Community healing
- Leadership model: rebel brands (Tesla, Audemars Piguet), ego-fuelers (Apple, Nike), community-builders (Nike, Lacoste), cultural remix leaders (Nike, Lacoste)

## Contradictions / Tensions
- The "omniconvenience" promise (ambient beauty that works without thought) sits in tension with the "curated self" promise (precise, intentional identity expression) — both valid but require different product/service architectures
- The leadership model draws on aspirational cases (Apple, Tesla) that are not beauty brands — translation to a heritage luxury beauty brand is a non-trivial leap
- The NCD data (70% of global deaths) creates a genuine opportunity but also an ethical risk: claiming therapeutic-grade effects from cosmetic products runs into regulatory and credibility constraints
