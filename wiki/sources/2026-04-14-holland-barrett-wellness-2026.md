## Summary

Holland & Barrett's fifth annual Wellness Trends Report (published Dec 2025 by their global team of scientists, nutritionists, forecasters, and innovators) identifies five macro wellness shifts for 2026. The framing: "It's time to ditch the guilt trip. We're not lazy. We're hormonal, inflamed and overloaded." A science-backed shift is underway from willpower-focused wellness to understanding the biological and metabolic roots of health challenges.

The five macro trends: 1) Over Willpower (GLP-1 and metabolic science reshaping self-image); 2) Back Your Body Now (proactive, preventive health optimization replacing reactive care); 3) Where Nature Meets Next-Gen Science (bioengineered ingredients, precision fermentation); 4) Joy of Wellness (anti-optimization backlash; humor and color replacing sterile health aesthetics); 5) Community as wellness infrastructure (social wellness — run clubs, group retreats — as behavior change mechanism).

The GLP-1 section is particularly data-rich: H&B reports a near-doubling of UK GLP-1 household penetration (2.3% → 4.1%), and 77% of UK GLP-1 users have experienced at least one side effect — creating demand for supplementary nutrition support stacks.

## Key Claims

- Global wellness market projected at $8.99 trillion by 2028
- GLP-1 usage is forecast to reach 70M patients by 2030 globally (Morgan Stanley)
- UK GLP-1 household penetration nearly doubled: 2.3% → 4.1% (Kantar 2025)
- 77% of UK GLP-1 users experienced at least one side effect — creating demand for support supplementation
- GLP-1 households saw average -5.5% grocery bill reduction in first six months (Cornell, 2024)
- 72% of UK adults struggle to get through the day (energy crisis signal)
- Only 17% of UK adults meet 5-a-day fruit/vegetable recommendation; 96% miss daily fibre targets
- Global gut health market expected to surpass $90B by 2030
- AI wellness platforms projected to grow to $24B by 2030 — AI as personalized health coach
- 84% of Gen Z prioritize a healthy lifestyle over career, personal enjoyment, or money
- 1 in 4 consumers don't know how to look after their brain health at different life stages
- 68% of Gen Z say wellness feels more "doable" with community support
- Social media misinformation: only 2.1% of nutrition content on TikTok aligns with public health guidelines
