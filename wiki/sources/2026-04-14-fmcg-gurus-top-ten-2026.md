## Summary

FMCG Gurus' annual consumer trend digest (food, beverage, and nutrition focus) frames 2026 as a world of "navigating contrast" — optimism with uncertainty, indulgence with intention, convenience with authenticity. The headline from FMCG Gurus' CEO: consumers are seeking brands that help them make sense of complexity through transparency, proof, and shared purpose. Value is no longer about price alone; it encompasses tangible benefits, ethical alignment, and emotional satisfaction.

Three priority areas dominate: trust and transparency (proof over promise, visible sourcing); mood and indulgence (emotional wellbeing; comfort food and escapism as stress management tools); and convenience reimagined (simplicity + health + personalization, not just speed). GLP-1 innovations, personalized nutrition, and mood-oriented products are highlighted as the most consequential category reshaping forces.

## Key Claims

- Consumers are seeking "proof over promise" — science-backed claims, clear sourcing, visible impact
- 7 in 10 consumers say small indulgences help them cope with financial stress (scrimp vs. splurge)
- Mood-enhancing and comfort-driven products remain key as busy lifestyles demand accessible escapism
- GLP-1 appetite management is reshaping FMCG product development across satiety, nutrition, and side-effect management
- Personalized nutrition is accelerating — consumers demand products tailored to individual health goals
- Convenience must be coupled with health, simplicity, and ease — "premium convenience" is the growth segment
- Sustainability: claims must be backed by visible, verified impact — virtue signaling is increasingly rejected

## Data Points

- 70% of consumers use small indulgences to cope with financial stress
- GLP-1 and related satiety innovations flagged as the single most disruptive FMCG trend for 2026
