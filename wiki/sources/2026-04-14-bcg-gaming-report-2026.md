## Summary

BCG's Video Gaming Report 2026 (based on a Global Gaming Survey of ~3,000 gamers plus industry leader conversations) declares that the long-awaited end to the three-year "video game winter" is imminent. Global gaming revenue is projected to grow from $263B (2025E) to $353B (2030E), a +4% CAGR — modest compared to the industry's 2010s doubling, but a recovery nonetheless.

The report frames four key structural forces: (1) GenAI opportunities and risks (AI for game content generation and player personalization); (2) Platform Evolution — console to cloud wars accelerating; (3) UGC (User-Generated Content) as the new creator economy within gaming; (4) App Store opening up (EU regulatory changes enabling alternative distribution). A fifth theme, Improving Monetization, addresses the "new math" of game pricing.

Key engagement finding: ~55% of gamers increased gaming time in the past six months. Youth pipeline is strong: 44% of gaming parents report their children playing by age 5, with Minecraft and Roblox (both UGC-heavy) as two of the three most popular first games. This signals that the next generation of gamers is being trained in participatory, creator-oriented gaming — not passive consumption.

## Key Claims

- Global gaming revenue: $254B (2024) → $263B (2025E) → $353B (2030E) at ~+4% CAGR
- Three-year "video game winter" ending; industry employment and investment stabilizing
- ~55% of gamers increased their gaming time in the past six months
- 44% of gaming parents report children playing by age 5 — strong youth pipeline
- Minecraft and Roblox (both UGC-heavy) among top 3 first games for children of gaming parents
- Mobile gaming remains the largest segment (~$128.7B in-app purchases, 2025E)
- Advertising in gaming: $31.9B in 2025E, growing at ~9% CAGR through 2030
- Cloud gaming streaming services: highest CAGR in the category (+69% through 2030) but from small base
- GenAI is creating both opportunities (content generation, personalization) and risks in gaming
- EU regulatory changes to app stores are enabling alternative distribution channels
- UGC platforms (Roblox, Minecraft) represent the "new creator economy" within gaming

## Data Points

- Survey: BCG Global Gaming Survey, ~3,000 gamers, published December 2025
- Global gaming revenue: $183B (2018) → $243B (2021, pandemic peak) → $236B (2022, post-pandemic dip) → $253B (2030E)
- Mobile in-app purchases: $128.7B (2025E, largest segment)
- Gaming advertising: $31.9B (2025E), +9% CAGR through 2030
- Cloud gaming: $0.6B (2025E), +69% CAGR to ~2030 (fastest growing sub-segment)
- Multigame subscription (console): +13% CAGR through 2030

## Contradictions / Tensions

- BCG is cautiously optimistic about the gaming recovery but acknowledges growth will not return to 2010s rates. This matters for gaming companies whose valuations were built on a "doubling per decade" growth thesis — moderate recovery will not satisfy investors expecting that trajectory.
- GenAI is flagged as both opportunity AND risk for gaming. Generative AI could enable smaller studios to compete with large publishers on content quality, disrupting the existing platform economics. The incumbents have the most to lose from democratized content creation.
