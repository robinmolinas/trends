## Summary

The New Consumer (Dan Frommer) and Coefficient Capital's annual consumer trends report — based on 14 proprietary surveys of 3,000+ US consumers, most recently November 2025 — identifies five transformational forces reshaping American commerce heading into 2026. The five: creator economy graduating to big business; GLP-1 medications as multi-sector transformation; longevity optimization going mainstream; wealth polarization creating "two Americas"; and AI-driven personalization as embedded infrastructure.

Despite the economic turbulence of 2025, the American consumer never stopped spending. Holiday e-commerce set new records: Black Friday at $11.8B (+9.1%), Cyber Monday at $14.3B (+7.1%), Nov 1–Dec 2 at $137.4B (+7.2%). The macro picture: optimism coexists with anxiety; health obsession coexists with pleasure-seeking; digital nativity coexists with a craving for authentic connection. The consumer is trading up where it matters — health, wellness, quality experiences — and trading down or sideways elsewhere.

## Key Claims

- 45% of Gen Z and Millennials consider themselves content creators; 40% want to pursue it as a career
- TikTok Shop projected to exceed $15B in US GMV in 2025 (second full year); top 1% of creators (8,450 individuals) drove 53% of $7.7B in video sales
- 23% of US households (~30M) have at least one GLP-1 user (up from 19% a year prior, Circana)
- GLP-1 users spend *more* on food/beverages post-medication: +36.8pp CPG food/bev spending after one year
- 91% of GLP-1 users feel like "a different person" since starting; 80% report better overall wellbeing
- GLP-1 users trade up: +51% net for fitness/wellness, +39% for energy drinks, +35% for beauty
- By 2030, 35% of food/beverage unit sales will come from GLP-1 households (up from 24% today)
- One-third of Americans in health "optimization mode" (43% of Gen Z/Millennials; 59% of GLP-1 users)
- 25% of blood test takers uploaded results to ChatGPT; 40% of Gen Z/Millennials did so
- US has ~24M millionaires (9% of adults); top 10% represent ~half of all consumer spending (Moody's)
- 46% would pay for AI-powered health tools; 53% of Gen Z/Millennials agree they could manage health better than doctor with right data/tools
- AI assistant traffic to US retail sites +760% YoY during Nov 1–Dec 1, 2024 shopping period
- 42% of consumers cite rising prices as most important US problem; 71% believe wealth gap is growing

## Data Points

- Survey: 3,000+ US consumers, November 2025 (14th edition of quarterly survey)
- Holiday 2025: Cyber Monday $14.3B (+7.1%), Black Friday $11.8B (+9.1%)
- TikTok Shop: US spending +52% YoY in Nov 2025; now surpasses Sephora and Ulta in transaction volume
- GLP-1 household penetration: US 23% (Sept 2025, Circana); UK share nearly doubled (2.3% → 4.1%, Kantar)

## Contradictions / Tensions

- "Two Americas" polarization creates brand strategy dilemma: premium and accessible premium are growing but the middle market is under pressure
- Wellness fatigue signal: 67% of Americans say "wellness culture today puts too much pressure on people to be perfect" — potential backlash to optimization culture
- TikTok Shop regulatory risk: the platform driving much of creator commerce remains subject to US regulatory action
