## Summary

The OECD’s *Strategic Foresight Toolkit for Resilient Public Policy* (published in 2025) offers a comprehensive, horizontal methodology to help policy makers assess the future-readiness and robustness of long-term strategies. In an increasingly volatile global landscape, the toolkit argues that traditional, single-point planning and siloed policymaking are insufficient. shunts and disruptions in one system (e.g., climate change or digital tech) inevitably cascade into others (e.g., social equity, supply chains, and geopolitics). 

To help governments navigate this complexity, the report identifies **25 possible future disruptions** organized into five categories (Environmental, Social, Technological, Economic, and Geopolitical). The toolkit outlines a structured stress-testing process designed to help governments identify hidden vulnerabilities, avoid resource misallocation, and discover new opportunities. The framework was developed under the OECD Horizontal Project on Building Climate and Economic Resilience and piloted with government partners in New Zealand, the Philippines, Canada, Indonesia, and Lithuania.

## Key Claims

- **The Global Green Divide**: Domestically successful net-zero and circular transitions in high-income countries can create a global divide. Firms in low- and middle-income countries face market exclusion due to inability to meet strict environmental standards, while bearing the brunt of climate catastrophes without adequate technology transfer.
- **Multilateralism Breakdown & Strategic Safety Nets**: Geopolitical rivalries between superpowers threaten global cooperation on climate goals, potentially leading to divided economic spheres and fragmented technology standards. Governments must prepare national safety nets (including stockpiles of critical inputs and redundant capabilities) to mitigate trade breakdown risks.
- **Horizontal Policy Stress-Testing**: Successful anticipatory governance requires evaluating policies not in isolation, but against combinations of multi-sectoral disruptions (e.g. Conspiracy Chaos + Hothouse Earth) to expose systemic weaknesses.

## Data Points

- **The 25 Disruptions**: BRIEFINGS provided on 25 distinct future scenarios including Hothouse Earth, Conspiracy Chaos (misinformation paralyzing democracy), Cyber Slowdown, Tech Titans (private companies displacing governments), and Multitrack World (globalization reversal).
- **Government Pilots**: The methodology was piloted with five country/municipality partners: New Zealand, the Philippines (NEDA), Calgary, Indonesia (Bappenas), and Lithuania (STRATA).
- **Transition Costs**: Built on findings from the OECD Net Zero+ synthesis report highlighting the structural economic adjustments of the net-zero shift.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- **Domestic Net-Zero vs. Global Sustainable Development**: Carbon reduction policies in high-income nations can undermine the economies of developing partners by raising import standards without supporting green tech transfer.
- **Interconnected Supply Chains vs. Sovereign Redundancy**: Global integration is highly cost-efficient, yet the risk of trade breakdown drives national governments toward costly, protectionist redundancy and stockpiling.
