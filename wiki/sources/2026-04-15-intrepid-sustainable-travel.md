## Summary
Commissioned by Intrepid Group (sustainable adventure travel operator), The Future Laboratory's 22-page report maps how the travel industry must transform from crisis mode to regenerative leadership. The report argues the industry is at a "tipping point" where climate and consumer behavioral shifts demand structural redesign, not incremental improvements. It forecasts travel trends to 2040 across five themes: carbon passports, chasing the shade (climate-driven migration of tourism), virtual vacations, raising "travel transformers" (next-gen climate-conscious travelers), and four future travel modalities.

## Key Claims
- **Carbon Passports**: Travelers receive annual carbon allowances governing how much they can fly — behavioral regulation through carbon budgeting at the individual level
- **Chasing the Shade**: Climate change is physically redirecting tourism as traditional hotspots become too hot, too dry, or too flooded. New "climate refuge" destinations emerge.
- **Virtual Vacations**: High-fidelity virtual travel as complementary (not replacing) physical travel — enables climate-constrained or mobility-limited people to experience places
- **Raising Travel Transformers**: Gen Z and Gen Alpha developing as deliberate travelers — learning, impact-driven, experiences over consumption
- **Four Future Travel Trends to 2040:**
  1. **People-positive Travel**: Community benefit and local economic integration as core product elements
  2. **Ephemeral Escapes**: Limited-access, authentic experiences that exist only temporarily — scarcity as value
  3. **Enhanced Eco-mobility**: Electric, hydrogen, and novel transport modes as premium sustainable alternatives
  4. **Real-time Footprints**: Dynamic carbon tracking integrated into booking and travel experience

## Contradictions / Tensions
- "Virtual Vacations" as complement vs. substitute — if they satisfy the travel urge effectively, they may cannibalize physical travel demand faster than operators expect
- Intrepid's position as an adventure/group tour operator may bias toward physical experiences over virtual or individual-mode alternatives
- Carbon passports require governance infrastructure that doesn't yet exist and faces political resistance in multiple major source markets
